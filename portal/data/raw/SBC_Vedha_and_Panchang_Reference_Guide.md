| 1. Introduction to Panchangam Parameters

In Vedic Astrology (Jyotish), the Panchangam is the five-fold astronomical calendar that maps out temporal quality based
on the relationship between the Sun and the Moon. These five core components are Tithi (Lunar Day), Vara (Weekday),
Nakshatra (Lunar Mansion), Yoga (Luni-Solar Angular Relationship), and Karana (Half of a Tithi). Understanding

these parameters allows an astrologer to construct electing times (Muhurtha) and analyze transits with systemic precision.

| 2. The Concept of Dagdha Rashi (Burned Signs)

The word Dagdha literally translates to "combust," "burnt," or "incinerated." In the spatial-temporal dynamics of the
Panchang, certain zodiac signs (Rashis) lose their vital capacity and structural integrity on specific lunar days (Tithis).

When a Tithi polarizes the sky, its corresponding Dagdha Rashis become highly inauspicious.

Any benevolent planet transiting through a Dagdha Rashi during its linked Tithi finds its powers suppressed, while
malefic planets become significantly more volatile. Astrologers strictly avoid initiating major lifetime milestones—such
as weddings, commercial inaugurations, or long-term financial commitments—when the Moon crosses through a sign

that is "burned" by the operational Tithi.

Important Temporal Exception: Both Purnima (Full Moon / 15th Tithi) and Amavasya (New Moon / 30th
Tithi) are intrinsically exempt from the Dagdha Rashi fault (Dosha). Because these days mark the absolute
crests of the lunar cycle, energy distribution remains total across the entire zodiac belt, leaving no sign
"burned."

| 3. The Architecture of the Sarvatobhadra Chakra

The Sarvatobhadra Chakra (SBC) is a multi-dimensional 9x9 square matrix containing exactly 81 smaller graphical
divisions (boxes). "Sarvatobhadra" translates to "wholesomely auspicious from all directions." This grid acts as a cosmic
radar screen, consolidating phonetics (vowels and consonants), solar days (Varas), lunar phases (Tithis), zodiac signs

(Rashis), and an expanded system of 28 Nakshatras.

Unlike the standard 27-Nakshatra system used in natal charts, the SBC integrates the intercalary constellation Abhijit
(located mathematically between Uttarashadha and Shravana). The 28 Nakshatras line the absolute outer border of the

9x9 grid, traveling in a precise clockwise layout starting from the top-left corner.

The Grid Composition
° 28 Nakshatras: Form the protective outer perimeter of the grid.
° 12 Rashis: Form the first inner ring immediately behind the Nakshatras.
° 16 Swaras (Vowels): Placed in the four corner hubs and the exact structural core of the matrix.

° 20 Vyanjanas (Consonants): Occupy the intermediary inner channels to represent the seed sounds of personal and

corporate names.

° Tithis & Varas: Mapped onto internal coordinates to cross-examine current calendar timings.

| 4. The Mechanics of Vedha (Planetary Piercing)

The fundamental operational engine of the SBC relies on the concept of Vedha, which means "piercing," "striking," or

"obstructing." When a planet transits through a specific Nakshatra along the outer border, it acts as a energetic broadcast

tower, firing lines of sight across the interior lanes of the 81-box matrix.

Any astrological or linguistic element resting on those intersecting coordinates is "pierced" by that planet's transiting

nature. A planet shoots its Vedha lines in three distinct vector paths simultaneously:

1. Frontal Vedha (Sammukha): A vector line shooting directly horizontal or vertical straight across the interior
axis of the grid.

2. Left Diagonal Vedha (Savya): A diagonal vector projecting forward along a 45-degree trajectory to the left side.

3. Right Diagonal Vedha (Apasavya): A diagonal vector projecting backward along a 45-degree trajectory to the
right side.

| 5. Planetary Rules and Motion Override Dynamics

Not all celestial bodies interact with the three directional vectors in an identical manner. The directional flow of a Vedha

is strictly modulated by the planet's intrinsic astronomical motion and state.

Directional Restrictions Based on Planet Type

° True Planets (Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn): Because these five physical bodies undergo all phases of
velocity—direct advancement, stationary pause, and apparent retrograde motion—they are fully capable of

influencing all three vectors (Frontal, Left Diagonal, and Right Diagonal) simultaneously.

° The Luminaries (Sun & Moon): The Sun and Moon travel with constant forward velocity and are mathematically
incapable of going retrograde. Consequently, they only cast the Frontal and Left Diagonal (Forward) Vedhas,
completely ignoring the backward right path.

° The Shadow Nodes (Rahu & Ketu): Rahu and Ketu are mathematical intersection points that inherently move
backward through the zodiac. Because their default state is retrograde, they only cast the Frontal and Right Diagonal

(Backward) Vedhas, leaving the forward left lane open.

The Motion Override Law

For the five true planets that possess multi-directional lines, the relative intensity of their vectors changes instantly based

on their speed and directional shifts in the sky:
° When a planet moves with Direct / Fast Motion, its forward-leaning Left Diagonal (Savya) vector becomes its
primary, highest-intensity output.

° When a planet enters Retrograde (Vakra) Motion, its energy is forced backward, causing its Right Diagonal

(Apasavya) vector to deliver the dominant, concentrated strike.

° When a planet becomes Stationary (Stambhana) as it readies to shift directions, its energy pools heavily into the

straight line, making its Frontal (Sammukha) vector incredibly sharp and disruptive.

| 6. Deciphering Functional Impact: Benefic vs. Malefic Vedha

A common misconception is that a "Vedha strike" is inherently negative. The geometric lines of the table are completely
neutral lines of sight. The structural impact—whether it brings windfalls or destruction—is determined solely by the

functional nature of the transiting planet casting the line:

Shubha Vedha (Benefic Piercing)

Triggered when a natural benefic planet—Jupiter, Venus, unafflicted Mercury, or a bright Waxing/Full Moon—
transits a stellar coordinate. Its lines of sight infuse the targeted Nakshatras, signs, and name-consonants with supportive,
expanding, and protective energy. This corresponds to unexpected financial success, clarity of mind, and the spontaneous

resolution of chronic problems.

Papa Vedha (Malefic Piercing)

Triggered when a natural malefic planet—Saturn, Mars, Rahu, Ketu, the Sun, or a dark Waning/New Moon—crosses
a coordinate. Its vectors project pressure, restrictions, or sudden structural breaks onto the recipient boxes. This manifests

as bureaucratic delays, sudden expenditure, physical injury, or professional friction.
