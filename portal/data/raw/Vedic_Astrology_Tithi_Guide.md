1. Understanding Tithi & Karyakatva

In Vedic Panchang, a Tithi represents a lunar day calculated by the 12-degree angular separation between the Sun and the
Moon. Karyakatva refers to the inherent purpose, behavioral qualities, and cosmic tasks assigned to these individual lunar
days, dictating their suitability for specific actions.

Calculation Formula: Tithi = (Moon's Longitude - Sun's Longitude) / 12°. Add 1 to the result to determine the current
operational Tithi (1 to 15) for either the Shukla (bright) or Krishna (waning) phase.

3. Dual Planetary Rulership Framework

Rulership shifts depending on the system. Under the Element View (Mahabhuta), Guru rules the Jaya group (3rd, 8th,
13th), while Saturn rules Purna. Under the Sequential View, planets cycle through individual days: Sun (1,9), Moon (2,10),
Mars (3,11), Mercury (4,12), Jupiter/Guru (5,13), Venus (6,14), Saturn (7,15), and Rahu (8,30). Therefore, for Dashami
(10th), the sequential ruler is the Moon, the deity is Yama, but it functions under Jupiter's expansion guidelines in electional
systems.
