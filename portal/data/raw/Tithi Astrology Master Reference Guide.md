Siddha Yoga: Matching Tithis and Weekdays

When a specific Tithi group falls on its corresponding planetary weekday, it manifests as Siddha Yoga (a day of highly successful outcomes). Conversely, some days create a clash:

Nanda Tithis falling on a Friday (ruled by Venus) \(\rightarrow \) Siddha Yoga.

Bhadra Tithis falling on a Wednesday (ruled by Mercury) \(\rightarrow \) Siddha Yoga.

Jaya Tithis falling on a Tuesday (ruled by Mars) \(\rightarrow \) Siddha Yoga.

Rikta Tithis falling on a Saturday (ruled by Saturn) \(\rightarrow \) Siddha Yoga.

Poorna Tithis falling on a Thursday (ruled by Jupiter) \(\rightarrow \) Siddha Yoga.

Latta and Latta Dosha (Disgrace)

Latta (Sanskrit: लत्ता), which literally means "kick", is a critical concept in Vedic astrology used primarily to analyze transits (Gocharas) and elect auspicious timings (Muhurtas). It refers to the special malefic mathematical aspect where a transiting planet "kicks" or afflicts a specific Nakshatra located at a fixed distance either ahead of or behind its current position. If this "kick" falls on a person’s Janma Nakshatra (birth star), it initiates Latta Dosha, causing major material, emotional, or physical setbacks.

 

This phenomenon is not found in the original four Vedas but is documented in authoritative Jyotish Vedanga treatises and Samhitas. [1, 2, 3]

The exact mapping of Latta, its mathematics, and its specific outcomes as validated by classical texts and renowned scholars is detailed below: [1]

The Mathematical Rules of Latta

Planets are divided into two categories based on the direction of their "kick": [1, 2]

Puro-Latta (Forward Kick): Malefic planets kick Nakshatras located ahead of them in regular zodiacal sequence.

Prashchat-Latta (Backward Kick): Benefic planets and Rahu kick Nakshatras located behind them in reverse sequence. [1, 2]

Scope of Application & Classical Commentary

Phaladeepika by Mantreswara (13th Century C.E.)

Transit Analysis (Gochara): Chapter 26 deals extensively with Nakshatra transits. Mantreswara highlights Latta as a primary hidden affliction that neutralizes otherwise positive planetary transits. If multiple planets kick the same star, the crisis escalates exponentially.

Muhurta Chintamani by Ramacharya (16th Century C.E.)

Electional Astrology (Muhurta): Dedicated chapters on marriage and travel note that a Muhurta Nakshatra must be completely free from Latta Dosha. A marriage or venture initiated on a "kicked" star will experience early decay.

Kalamrita by Kalidasa

General Transit Neutralization: This text describes exceptions to the rule. For example, it explains that if a planet causing Latta is retrograde, or if the ruling lord of the birth star is exceptionally strong, the severity of the "kick" is significantly diminished.

Dr. B.V. Raman(Renowned 20th-century Astrologer & Author)

Practical Application: In works such as Muhurta or Electional Astrology, Dr. Raman emphasizes that while common planetary strengths (like exaltation) are visible, subtle energetic blemishes like Latta can quietly derail personal undertakings if they strike the native's Janma Nakshatra.

🛠️ How to Read and Use the Ghaat Chakra

To use this table, locate your Birth Moon Sign (Janma Rashi) in the far-left column. All other elements in that specific row represent your unique structural vulnerabilities.

Ghaat Chandrama (Moon): When the transiting Moon enters this specific sign relative to yours (e.g., when the Moon transits Virgo for a Taurus native), your mental clarity drops. Avoid making high-stakes emotional or life decisions during these 2.25-day windows.

Ghaat Tithi & Vaar: Avoid scheduling highly auspicious events—such as weddings, signing business contracts, buying property, or undergoing optional major surgeries—on your specific Ghaat lunar day class or weekday.

Ghaat Nakshatra: The day the Moon transits your designated Ghaat star represents a period of low vitality. It is traditionally used for internal rest, spiritual contemplation, or routine tasks rather than aggressive public launches.

Ghaat Lagna: If you are selecting a precise hour (Muhurta) for an event, ensure the rising sign (Lagna) of that hour does not match either your Samaan (Same) or Vipareet (Opposite) Ghaat Lagnas listed in the final columns.

📜 Validation from Classical Texts

Classical texts emphasize that the Ghaat Chakra is not a permanent curse, but a rhythmic cosmic cycle of low energy. The Phaladeepika notes that while general transits might appear favorable on a standard calendar, an overlapping alignment with one's personal Ghaat elements can cause unexpected friction or delays.
