Reader’s orientation

This handbook separates three things that are often mixed up: (1) the time division called Do-Ghati Muhurta, (2) the fixed muhurta-lord Nakshatra mapping used by Panchang systems, and (3) the practical purpose/avoidance guidance derived from muhurta and Nakshatra election principles. It is a reference guide, not a substitute for a full individual muhurta chosen with Tithi, Vara, Yoga, Karana, actual lunar Nakshatra, Lagna, Tara-bala, Chandra-bala and purpose-specific rules.

1. What Do-Ghati Muhurta Means

Do-Ghati Muhurta literally means a two-ghati time block. In the traditional Panchang model, a civil day is reckoned from local sunrise to the next sunrise. The daytime from sunrise to sunset is divided into 30 ghatis, and the nighttime from sunset to the next sunrise is divided into 30 ghatis. Therefore one Do-Ghati Muhurta is two ghatis: 1/15 of the daytime or 1/15 of the nighttime, depending on whether the slot is a day or night slot. [S1]

Do not assume every muhurta is exactly 48 minutes. For example, on 30-May-2026 in Athlone, the day slots are about 66m 26s each and the night slots are only about 29m 30s each because summer daylight is long at that latitude.

Vedic/Brahmana background

Texts such as the Taittiriya Brahmana and Satapatha Brahmana preserve muhūrta as an ancient time measure and ritual time concept. This supports the time-unit foundation, not every later election rule. [S4]

Primary-text background

Panchang practice

Modern Panchang references describe the day as sunrise-to-sunrise, split day/night into ghatis, and list the 30 Do-Ghati Muhurtas with auspicious/inauspicious status. [S1]

Purpose interpretation

3. Calculation Method Used

Step

Action

Note

1

Get local sunrise, sunset and next sunrise for the chosen date and place.

Astronomical apparent sunrise/sunset; local timezone applied.

2

Day muhurta duration = (sunset − sunrise) ÷ 15.

Slots 1–15.

3

Night muhurta duration = (next sunrise − sunset) ÷ 15.

Slots 16–30; past-midnight times belong to the same Panchang day.

4

Assign fixed Muhurta name and fixed Nakshatra mapping by slot number.

The Nakshatra shown in tables is the muhurta-lord mapping, not necessarily the Moon’s actual Nakshatra at that minute.

5

Round display times to nearest minute.

Underlying calculations were done to seconds; table times are for easy reference.

Nakshatra mapping vs actual Moon Nakshatra

In Do-Ghati tables, the Nakshatra column is a fixed muhurta-lord mapping: slot 1 corresponds to Ardra, slot 2 to Ashlesha by day / Purva Bhadrapada by night, and so on. This is different from the Moon’s real-time Nakshatra on that date. For a full election, both should be checked.

7. Practical Election Checklist

#

Filter

What to do

1

Purpose fit

Choose a slot whose muhurta, Nakshatra nature and status match the action. Do not use a sharp/dreadful slot for a gentle ceremony unless there is a compelling reason.

2

Panchanga shuddhi

Check Tithi, Vara, actual lunar Nakshatra, Yoga and Karana. A good Do-Ghati slot does not override a badly afflicted full Panchanga.

3

Tara-bala

Compare the day’s actual lunar Nakshatra with the person’s Janma Nakshatra for personal suitability.

4

Chandra-bala

Check the Moon’s transit from the person’s Janma Rashi; avoid difficult placements for important elections.

5

Lagna

For major works, choose a strong rising sign and avoid lagna tyajya, afflicted lagna lord and purpose-specific doshas.

6

Sandhya awareness

The beginnings and endings of day/night are traditionally reserved for worship, transition and purification; avoid rushing worldly launches there unless required.

7

When forced

Use the principle of more good than harm: select the least problematic slot, strengthen the intent with prayer/remedies and avoid known tyajya/visha portions.

8. Glossary

Term

Meaning

Ghati

Traditional time division. In fixed modern conversion, 1 ghati is often treated as 24 minutes; in Panchang day/night division it varies by date and place.

Do-Ghati

Two ghatis; one muhūrta slot in this system.

Muhurta

A selected or measured time segment; in this handbook, one of the 30 Do-Ghati divisions.

Nakshatra

Lunar mansion/star division. In tables here, the Nakshatra shown is the fixed muhurta-lord mapping, not necessarily the Moon’s actual Nakshatra.

Tara-bala

Personal strength or compatibility of a day’s actual Nakshatra relative to a person’s birth Nakshatra.

Chandra-bala

Suitability based on the Moon’s transit position relative to the person’s birth Moon sign.

Panchanga

Five limbs of traditional calendar analysis: Tithi, Vara, Nakshatra, Yoga and Karana.
