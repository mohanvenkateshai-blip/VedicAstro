For many decades, we have beer: doing research in the use of
different dashas given in Parashari and Jaimini texts. While now some
of the Jaimini dashas have come into effective use, the nakshatra
dashas of Parashara needed extensive and original research to
discover effective predictive tools.

I had asked N.K.Sharma and }hanjhi to do some work on
Yogini Dasha many years ago and they produced a.book which has
been in use now.

The fascination of Yogini dasha is that it seems to be the best of
dashas for timing events even in cases where the uncertainty of birth
time with slight error can give degrees of Moon which would need
rectification. In the case of Yogini dasha, with the unrectified degrees
of Moon, events can still be times with remarkable closeness.

A controversy arose in the case of Yogini mainly because of its
shortness it being a dasha of only thirty six years. Life is not a story of
exact replication of events of life of the first thirty six years in the next
thirty six years. How is this riddle to be unravelled?

In a research group of Bharatiya Vidya Bhawan, 1 discussed this
with students and suggested many approaches which were:

1. If the first cycle of Yogini Dasha starts with janma nakshatra or the
birth constellation, the second one could possibly start with
Lagna or Sun etc. It would then give us different starting points
for each of the cycle of Yogini Dasha. Will it solve the problem?

2. Could we follow the Chara Dasha pattern and keep moving in
Yogini Dasha in a systematic and chronological order as the
nakshatras as spread on the zodiac? May be this could solve the
problem of not repeating mechanically the events of the first
thirty six years of one’s life.

3. The third method could be repeating the order of Yogini Dasha
exactly as it is but changing the starting point of the second and
third cycles from the next nakshatra so as not to the same
horoscopic pattern for interpretation. This needs explaining. If in
the case of a person born with Chitra Nakshatra, the starting

Yogini dasha ruling at the time of epoch is based on the Nakshatra
(Star) occupied by Moon. The Nakshatra number is counted from
Ashwini, which is taken as one. Add three to the Nakshatra number
so arrived. Divide the sum by eight. Ignore the quotient. The
remainder obtained represent the Yogini dasha operating at the time of
epoch. The remainder number is the Sr. No. of Table I. Abhijit
nakshatra is not taken into account.

Balance of Dasha at birth

Full Dasha period of a yogini spans one Nakshatra. The degrees
remaining to be traversed by Moon in the Nakshatra occupied is the
proportional balance of Dasha at birth/epoch.

Sub periods

Major periods of each yogini Dasha is further divided into eight
sub periods corresponding to eight yoginis. All eight yoginis divide
each major period in proportion to their individual major period. The
first sub period belong to major Dasha lord and other periods follow in
given order.

A simple rule for finding the duration of sub period of P yogini in
major period of Q yogini is;

Sub period = Dasha years of Q x Dasha period of P x 10 are the
number of days of sub period.

Calculation Example

1. Dasha at birth — Moon is in Cancer at 12 degree 40 min. It is in
Pushya Nakshatra. Pushya is 8" Nakshatra from Ashwini. Add 3
to 8, which gives 11. Divide 11 by 8. Remainder is 3. Third Dasha
is of Dhanya and is the Dasha at birth.

2. Balance at birth — Pushya Nakshatra is from 3 deg. 20 min. to

16-deg. 40 min. The span is 13 deg. 20 min. From 12 deg. 40
min. moon is yet to cover 4 deg. 0 min. to reach end of Pushya.
Dasha period of Dhanya is 3 years.
Therefore balance of Dhanya will be 4 deg. 0 min. multiplied by
three (Full period of Dasha) and divided by 13 deg. 20 min. This is
converted into years, Months and days to give 10 Months 24
Days as balance of Dhanya Dasha.

3. Order of Dasha — Dasha at birth is Dhanya and the order is
Dhanya, BhraMari, Bhadrika, Ulka, Siddha, Sankata, Mangla,
Pingla and again Dhanya.

4. Sub periods — Suppose we want to know Siddha sub period in
Ulka major. Multiply 6 by 7, which give 42. Again multiply it by
10. Result 420 is the number of days or one year two Months as
the sub period of Siddha in Ulka major period and vice-versa.
