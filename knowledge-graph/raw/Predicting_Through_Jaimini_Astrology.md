Jaimini system of astrology is a unique. It is different

from the general system of astrology which is Parashar
system. Jaimini system has its advantages. When any
astrologer combines the two systems then he can have a
wider understanding of astrology.

Parashar system and these are listed below. These are
different approach to the same problem. In a way these
are not different astrology but are different approach.

1.

The aspects are based on signs and not planets. The

independently.

The significator or karakas are fixed in Parashar
system. The karakas are variable in Jaimini.

The signs are synonymous with houses in Jaimini.
In Parashar the house and sign are distinct.

Most of dasha in Parashar are dependent on the
longitude of moon. The dasha of Jaimini are not
based on moon.

The calculation of strength of planets and houses
are elaborate in Parashar and need detail
calculations. This calculation is simple in Jaimini.

Concept of Karkamsha is unique.

The trikonas or the trines from ascendant are the
best benefic signs. The lords of trines are always
considered Benefics. Trines are called laxmi sthana and
also the dharma trines.

There are three methods of computing trikona dasha.
We will discuss all three but we give first the most used
and tried method.

Applicability

Both Parashar and Jaimini have not assigned any
conditions to be present in a horoscope to qualify it for
the application of this dasha. They have said that this
dasha commences from the strongest sign in the trine. It
is the strongest of ascendant, fifth house and the ninth
house. A sign is stronger when occupied by a planet.
Therefore it can be inferred that this dasha is more suited
or applicable when the trines of a horoscope are occupied
by planets. The question now arises as to how many
planets should be in trines and do we include Rahu and
Ketu also as planets?

Trines have three signs and some infer that all three
signs should be occupied or at least three planets except
Rahu and Ketu are in trines not necessarily all the trines.
However the strengths of signs can be assessed even when
there are no planets in a sign.

We will now study the three methods of writing the
trikona dasha.
