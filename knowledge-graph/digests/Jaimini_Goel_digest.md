# Predicting Through Jaimini Astrology (V.P. Goel) — Structured Digest

## Bibliographic Metadata
- **Title:** Predicting Through Jaimini Astrology
- **Author:** V.P. Goel (Vinod Prakash Goel) — former Indian Engineering Services officer; M.Tech IIT Kharagpur; Gold Medalist Jyotish Alankar & Jyotish Acharya, Bhartiya Vidya Bhavan
- **Publisher:** Vani Publications (Hindu Astrology Series)
- **Source file:** extracted_markdown/Predicting_Through_Jaimini_Astrology.md (9,257 lines)
- **Total chapters:** 10
- **Scope:** This is a PRACTICAL application guide to Jaimini astrology. Goel explicitly states the book "gives a practical approach to Jaimini predictions" and that "secrets of predicting are revealed for the first time." The author, being an engineer, presents methods in step-by-step fashion with worked example charts. The book covers 10 distinct Jaimini dasha systems (Chara, Mandook, Sthira, Brahma, Narayan, Navamsha, Narayan Shoola, Trikon, Atmanadi) with multiple calculation variants per dasha, and a dedicated chapter on Argala with house-by-house example analysis. It is designed to complement (not replace) the Parashari system and to enable event-prediction even when birth time is uncertain (since Chara karakas and lagna sign are stable against small time errors).

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

### Chapter 1: Basics (Pages 5–28)
Foundational differences between Jaimini and Parashari systems: sign-based aspects (movable→fixed except adjacent; fixed→movable except adjacent; common→other common); variable chara karakas (7 planets ranked by longitude); signs synonymous with houses; dashas not moon-based; Karkamsha concept; Argala. Defines the 7 karakas (AK, Amk, BK, MK, PK, GK, DK) with significations. Introduces Pada/Arudha computation, Karkamsha Lagna (KL), Swamsha, Jaimini Yogas (14 listed), planetary and sign strength (Graha Bala & Rashi Bala with 5 sources each), Brahma/Maheswara/Rudra identification, VKHL (Vridhi Karaka Hora Lagna), Parashar Hora Lagna, Ghatika Lagna, Varnada Lagna, Dwara/Bahya Rashi, and the core examination framework for any dasha. **Contains Goel's key original disclosure: the house-lord relationship rule for dasha prediction.**

### Chapter 2: Argala in Prediction (Pages 25–42)
Defines Argala ("lock") — planets in 4th, 2nd, 11th cause argala; 10th, 12th, 3rd obstruct; 5th causes argala, 9th obstructs. Three+ malefics in 3rd = Vipreet Argala (very favorable, no counter). Argala strength comparison rule. Quarter-based longitude obstruction (1st quarter obstructed by 4th, 2nd by 3rd, etc.). Nodes counted in reverse. BPHS-derived effects. **Provides a full house-by-house Argala analysis of Indira Gandhi's chart** plus example charts for longevity (Harivanshrai Bachchan), accident, position (Obama), and fame (King George VI). Argala is applicable to dasha prediction by making the running dasha sign the lagna.

### Chapter 3: Chara Dasha (Pages 39–70)
The "most important Jaimini Rashi dasha" — "like Vimshottari dasha of Jaimini." Presents **three calculation methods**: Method 1 (K.N. Rao / Vemuri Rama Murthy Shastri) — starts from lagna, order based on 9th house sign (direct/indirect groups), dasha years by counting to lord minus one, special dual-lordship rules for Scorpio (Mars+Ketu) and Aquarius (Saturn+Rahu). Method 2 (Dr. P.S. Shastri) — adds exaltation/debilitation adjustment, prefers sign strength over degrees. Method 3 (Irangati Rangacharya) — different for male/female, uses padakrama order, no deduction of one year. Validates all three methods against Srimavo Bandaranaike, Rajesh Khanna, and Indira Gandhi events (PM appointment, marriage, death).

### Chapter 4: Mandook Dasha (Pages 67–103)
"Frog-jump" dasha. Presents **six methods**: Method 1 (BVB/K.N. Rao) — requires 4+ planets in Kendras (excl. Rahu/Ketu), starts from lagna (odd) or 7th (even). Method 2 (P.S. Shastri/BPHS) — universal, starts from stronger of lagna/7th, fixed periods (7/8/9 years for movable/fixed/dual). Method 3 (Sanjay Rath/Updesha Sutras) — starts from odd sign of lagna or its adarsh sign, every 3rd sign. Method 4 (Irangati Rangacharya) — padakrama from stronger of lagna/7th (male) or lagna/4th (female). Method 5 (Labh Mandook) — on Ekadashamsha, ayur dasha of war/destruction. Method 6 — trine groups, gender-specific. Validates methods 1 & 2 against M.S. Subbulakshmi, Naina Devi, Pranav Mukherjee, and a no-planets-in-angles chart.

### Chapter 5: Sthira & Brahma Dasha (Pages 100–127)
Sthira dasha — for misfortunes, death, miseries but usable for all events. Starts from sign occupied by Brahma. Uses Graha/Rashi Bala (short BVB method vs. full Jaimini method compared). Fixed periods (7/8/9 years). Always direct order. Brahma dasha (Irangati Rangacharya method) — for longevity/death timing; starts from Brahma's sign (odd) or 7th from it (even); periods counted from sign to 6th lord. Validates against example chart 1, Charan Singh, Swami Vivekananda, Swami Prabhupad. Conclusion: "Brahma dasha is useful for indicating the time of death."

### Chapter 6: Narayan Dasha (Pages 124–142)
Important predictive dasha, universally applicable. Starts from stronger of lagna/7th. Order based on 9th house (direct/indirect groups). Sequence depends on starting sign nature: movable=regular, fixed=6th sign jumps, dual=trine (5th sign) jumps. Dasha years by counting to lord minus one, with Scorpio/Aquarius dual-lordship rules. Validates against Indira Gandhi (birth of children, husband's death, PM, her death), Vladimir Putin (PM rise), Ariel Sharon (PM, death), Saddam Hussein (rise, fall, execution), Aurobindo Ghosh (samadhi).

### Chapter 7: Navamsha Dasha (Pages 139–165)
Confirmation tool for results from other dashas. Presents **three methods**: Method 1 — applied on navamsha chart, starts from navamsha lagna (odd) or 7th (even), always direct order. Method 2 — navamsha chart, single lordship (Ketu rules Scorpio, Rahu rules Aquarius). Method 3 (Updesha Sutras) — uses birth chart, adarsh sign for even lagna, fixed 9-year periods. Validates all three against Indira Gandhi (birth of sons, husband's death, father's death, mother's death, son's death, her death) and Narendra Modi (PM). Includes a dedicated section on marriage timing via navamsha dasha.

### Chapter 8: Narayan Shoola Dasha / NSD (Pages 162–182)
Nirayana Shoola Dasha — for longevity. Starts from stronger of lagna/7th. Always direct order. Each dasha = 9 years (total 108 years = full lifespan). 12 sub-periods of 9 months each. **Examined in both birth chart and Varanasi Trimshamsha (D30).** Provides 10 observations for identifying fatal periods: benefic/lord association = protection; Saturn relation delivers fatal blow; Rahu/Ketu in 6/8/12; Mars association = sudden end (accident/war); Trimshamsha confirmation = death with problems; birth chart confirmation = normal death; Rudra/Maheswara relation; 2nd/7th house maracas. Validates against Indira Gandhi, a 1899-born chart, Amrita Pritam, Charan Singh, Sanjay Gandhi.

### Chapter 9: Trikon Dasha (Pages 179–216)
Trines (1/5/9) are Lakshmi Sthhana / dharma trines. Presents **three methods**: Method 1 (BVB, similar to B.V. Raman) — starts from strongest trine sign, moves in trine groups, order based on 9th house. Method 2 (Irangati Rangacharya/Vriddha Karika) — gender-specific, frontal (sammukha) sign for even strongest, padakrama order. Method 3 (Jagannath Hora) — direct/indirect based on starting sign odd/even. Special rules: 7th lord = 10 years; exalted +1 year; debilitated −1 year. Validates against a female lottery winner ($35M windfall), an accident chart, Queen Elizabeth II (queen), Queen Victoria (queen), Indira Gandhi (PM), Bill Clinton (president), King George VI (king). Conclusion: best for timing achievements; trikona jump is essential.

### Chapter 10: Atmanadi Dasha (Pages 213–231)
Used for timing of death. Irangati Rangacharya method (also called Hora dasha). Always starts from lagna. Direct order for odd lagna, indirect for even. Periods by counting to lord (direct for odd sign, reverse for even); 12 years if lord in sign or 12th; 10 years if lord in 7th. Examination rules: MD = dwara rashi (examine lord's associations/aspects); bahya rashi examined; malefic association, malefic in Kendra, or DK = bad. Validates against K.M. Munshi, Amrita Pritam, Charan Singh, Sanjay Gandhi, Ariel Sharon, Saddam Hussein, M.S. Subbulakshmi — all death events.

## Practical Karaka Application

- **Atmakaraka (AK) in prediction:** AK is "like king" — the most important planet; its strength/placement indicates the horoscope's overall strength. Represents soul, self, physical appearance, first house. Kendriya bala (angular position from AK) is the basis for planetary strength in Jaimini. AK's navamsha sign = Karkamsha Lagna (treated as prime lagna, more important than lagna). Events are confirmed when dasha sign relates to AK. In death timing, AK's placement from dasha sign matters.
- **Amatyakaraka (Amk) in career:** Amk rules karmas, 2nd/10th house, career, wealth, social status. "Death giver to spouse and self." Goel's rule: when Amk is in angles/trines/11th from AK (in birth or navamsha) = success with less effort; in trik houses = success after hard work. Amk in 3rd/6th with Mars/Sun/Saturn/Rahu/Ketu (or aspected by them) = professional success. 2nd/4th/5th from Amk having auspicious planets = high status. In career prediction from dasha, the 10th house from dasha is examined for Amk relation; 11th house from dasha for gains.
- **Darakaraka (DK) in marriage:** DK governs spouse, partners, 7th house, "giver of death." In marriage timing: dasha/antar dasha sign should relate to DK; 7th house/lord from dasha should relate to DK; 7th pada from dasha sign clinches prediction. Goel's navamsha dasha marriage rule: if 7th house/lord from dasha also relates to PK, it indicates marriage of choice OR children born early. DK aspected by Rahu/Ketu in dasha = marital problems.
- **Bhatrikaraka (BK):** siblings and father; 3rd/9th house; Guru/preceptors; can cause happiness/harm to parents.
- **Matrikaraka (MK):** 4th house — mother, home, conveyance, schooling, property, culture; maraca for father.
- **Putrakaraka (PK):** 5th house — intelligence, higher education, creativity, good luck, students, profession changes. PK + DK relation in dasha = marriage of choice or early children.
- **Gnati karaka (GK):** 6th/8th/12th houses — relations, external sources, trouble-maker, diseases, arguments, service problems, rivals, spiritual sadhana. GK aspecting dasha sign often shows illness/obstacles.
- **Seven vs. Eight Karakas:** Goel firmly uses 7 karakas (excludes Rahu/Ketu). States that 8-karaka scheme only arises if longitude is taken in minutes (not seconds) and is "not possible" with precise calculation. Notes Rahu is sometimes suggested for 8th karaka but he sticks to 7 "as it gives good results."

## Chara Dasha — Practical Prediction Rules

- **How to judge a Chara Dasha period (Goel's method):**
  1. Make the running dasha sign the lagna (treat it as ascendant).
  2. Examine the dasha sign, chara karakas (and their navamsha signs), lord of the sign, position of Padas, yogas, and aspects on the dasha sign.
  3. Aspects carry lordship AND karaka significations — combinations of karaka produce different results.
  4. A good placement and aspect of the relevant karaka from the dasha sign gives good results of that karaka's significations.
- **House judgment from Dasha sign (Goel's KEY original rule — "disclosed for the first time in this book"):**
  The lord of the house under consideration plays an important role. The lord will meet one of two conditions:
  (a) The lord IS the chara karaka of the event (e.g., 7th lord = DK for marriage), OR
  (b) The lord is aspecting the dasha sign or the house of the event.
  Goel states: "This principle is disclosed for the first time in this book. It is an important tool which is not discussed by any author till date."
- **Event-timing rule:** "Events will happen in the sign of pada and the sign of the karaka." E.g., if Jupiter is DK, marriage events happen in Sagittarius or Pisces dasha (or exaltation/debilitation signs of Jupiter). If Jupiter also relates to these signs, "it is almost confirmed." The 7th pada from dasha sign clinches the prediction.
- **Special rules / shortcuts:**
  - It is difficult to time events from karaka-dasha relation alone, because in any MD at least 4 antardashas will relate to the karaka; including navamsha and upapada multiplies the problem. Therefore the **house-lord rule** above is the discriminator.
  - Dual lordship: Ketu co-rules Scorpio, Rahu co-rules Aquarius. When Mars/Ketu (or Saturn/Rahu) are in different signs, count to the stronger (more associations → higher degrees → minutes → seconds).
  - For Scorpio/Aquarius dasha periods, special counting rules apply (see Ch 3).
  - Sub-periods: as many months as MD years; MD keeps last (Method 1) or first (Methods 2/3) sub to itself; order follows MD order.
- **Three-lagna confirmation:** Results confirmed from Karkamsha Lagna (real promise), Lagna (aspirations), and Pada Lagna (public image). Wealth from KL = truly wealthy; from lagna = wants to be wealthy; from pada lagna = seen as wealthy. "Any person if wealthy from pada lagna but poor from Karkamsha lagna then it is show business."

## Argala — Practical Use

- **Definition (Goel's interpretation):** "Argala means lock. A lock is used for safe keeping of articles. It can also be used to prevent something to escape." Argala decides mutual relation of houses, signs, and planets. "The real picture emerges when we apply the Argala."
- **Types (Goel's classification):**
  1. **Primary Argala:** planets in 4th, 2nd, 11th cause argala — obstructed by 10th, 12th, 3rd respectively.
  2. **Secondary Argala:** planets in 5th cause argala — obstructed by 9th.
  3. **Vipreet Argala:** three or more malefics in 3rd house = very favorable, NO counter.
  4. **Quarter-based obstruction (Goel's specific rule):** 1st quarter (0°–7°30′) argala obstructed by 4th quarter (22°30′–30°); 2nd quarter (7°30′–15°) by 3rd quarter (15°–22°30′); etc. Planet not in corresponding quarter does not obstruct.
  5. **Nodes:** argala/obstruction counted in REVERSE direction for Rahu/Ketu.
- **Strength rule:** If argala-causing planets are stronger (in number/quality) than obstructing planets, argala prevails.
- **Effects with example charts (key examples):**
  - **Indira Gandhi (full house-by-house analysis):** Lagna gets unobstructed argala from Jupiter (11th) + Sun/Mercury (5th), all aspecting lagna → "famous and a king." 2nd house argala from Sun/Mercury prevails despite Jupiter's obstruction (more planets) → wealth from Sun's sources. 7th house gets unobstructed argala from retrograde Jupiter, Sun, Mercury → "abundant wealth, famous and marital happiness." 8th house: unobstructed argala from Venus/Rahu and Ketu → "Her end was sudden and she had a medium life span." On planets: Sun gets argala from Venus/Rahu (6th) → father died in Scorpio dasha due to illness/mental agony. Mars gets argala from Venus/Rahu and Ketu (2nd house) → Leo dasha (1983–1992), she and son Rajiv were killed; "Mars is violent."
  - **Harivanshrai Bachchan (longevity):** 8th house gets unobstructed argala from exalted Jupiter + Moon (both strong) → long life.
  - **Accident chart (30 Apr 1945):** 6th house gets unobstructed argala from Ketu, Venus, Mercury, Mars; from KL, 6th house (Cancer) gets argala from Moon → serious road accident Sept 1972 (Aries dasha, which gets argala from Saturn=GK and Rahu).
  - **Barack Obama (position):** Lagna + 7th house get unobstructed argala AND aspect from Moon → fame and position combination; KL (Sagittarius) gets unobstructed argala from Jupiter (its lord) → two-term US President.
  - **King George VI (fame):** 7th from lagna/Arudha lagna gets argala from Jupiter + Ketu; Ketu aspects 7th → "fame came through significations of Ketu."
- **Argala cancellation (obstruction rules):**
  - 10th house planets cancel 4th house argala; 12th cancels 2nd; 3rd cancels 11th; 9th cancels 5th.
  - Quarter-mismatch = no obstruction.
  - No counter exists for Vipreet Argala (3+ malefics in 3rd).
  - If argala planets are stronger/more numerous, argala prevails over obstruction.
- **Goel's extension — Argala on houses (beyond BPHS):** Consider the relation of the argala-causing planet to the house lord and karaka. If the planet is inimical to the house, it harms; otherwise helps. Same applies to obstructing planet. Exception: special argala from 3rd house. Also consider the signification of the house FROM WHERE argala is caused (e.g., 11th house argala = gain/loss to the target house). When the argala-causing planet is ALSO the lord of the house, "the effect of argala is many fold." Unobstructed argala of Rahu/Ketu gives "mystery" to the significations.

## Pada / Upapada in Practice

- **Pada computation (Goel's method):** Count from the house to its lord; again count the same number from the lord. The resulting sign is the Arudha (Pada) of that house. Example: Lagna lord Mercury in 9th → count 9 from Mercury → Capricorn = Arudha of lagna. Goel states: "We are not using exceptions for calculating the Arudha" — i.e., he does NOT apply the classical exception that when lord is in 1st/7th from house, the 10th from it is taken. He acknowledges a strict longitudinal method exists (difference of house longitude to lord longitude, added to lord longitude) but notes it "needs further research" and uses the simple counting method.
- **Pada Lagna (Arudha of 1st house):** Also called paka lagna. "Reflection of lagna" — the image of the person as perceived by others. When Pada Lagna = Lagna itself, the person is "considered famous by outside world also."
- **Upapada (Arudha of 12th house):** "Jaimini recommends use of up pada for all relations." Used prominently for marriage/partnership prediction. Goel examines UP in dasha signs for marriage timing — e.g., Charan Singh marriage in Aquarius/Scorpio sthira dasha: "Aquarius has DKn and aspected by UP" and "Scorpio is aspected by Dk and UP."
- **Upapada for marriage timing (Goel's approach):**
  - The 7th pada from the dasha sign "clinches the prediction" for marriage.
  - In navamsha dasha marriage analysis: 7th house/lord from dasha should relate to DK; if also to PK → marriage of choice or early children.
  - Example Srimavo Bandaranaike (marriage 1940): navamsha dasha Capricorn/Pisces; Capricorn aspected by DK Sun; 7th lord from Capricorn (Moon) aspects Capricorn; Pisces occupied by PK.
  - Example Rajesh Khanna (marriage Mar 1973): navamsha dasha Leo/Taurus; 7th lord from Leo IS the DK (in 10th from Leo); Taurus has DK; 7th from Taurus contains PK Mars which is 7th lord from Taurus.
  - Example Indira Gandhi (marriage 26 Mar 1942): navamsha dasha Capricorn/Capricorn; 7th lord from Capricorn (Moon) aspects Cancer; Capricorn aspected by DK.
- **Example charts (key findings):**
  - Indira Gandhi: Pada Lagna analysis shows public image vs. inner reality distinction (KL Capricorn with Jupiter's unobstructed argala → "really famous").
  - Pranav Mukherjee: Marriage in Cancer/Taurus mandook dasha — Taurus has DK in 10th from it, aspected by UP.
  - Charan Singh: Marriage in Aquarius/Scorpio sthira dasha — both signs aspected by DK and UP.

## Goel's Interpretive Shortcuts (beyond classical sutras)

1. **House-Lord Dasha Rule (Goel's primary original disclosure):** For any event in a dasha, the lord of the relevant house (from dasha sign) must EITHER be the chara karaka of that event OR aspect the dasha sign / house of event. Goel states this is "not discussed by any author till date" and is "an important tool." This cuts through the ambiguity of karaka-dasha relations.
2. **Three-Lagna Image/Reality Distinction:** Karkamsha Lagna = real inner quality (the promise); Lagna = aspirations/desires; Pada Lagna = public image. The "show business" rule: wealthy from Pada Lagna but poor from KL = show business (image only).
3. **Pada + Karaka Sign Event Timing:** Events happen in the dasha of the sign of the pada and the sign of the karaka. The 7th pada from dasha sign clinches the prediction (especially marriage).
4. **Quarter-Based Argala Obstruction:** Argala obstruction requires the obstructing planet to be in the matching quarter of its sign (1st↔4th, 2nd↔3rd). A planet not in the matching longitude does not obstruct — a refinement not in the classical sutras.
5. **Vipreet Argala:** Three or more malefics in 3rd house cause a special argala that is "very favorable" and has NO counter — distinct from ordinary argala rules.
6. **Nodes in Reverse for Argala:** Counting of argala and obstruction is in reverse direction for Rahu/Ketu.
7. **Nodes in Kartari:** Goel includes Rahu/Ketu in Kartari formation (classical is ambiguous). Also includes them for aspects and predictive purposes.
8. **Argala-causing Planet = House Lord = Multiplied Effect:** When the planet causing argala is also the lord of the house, "the effect of argala is many fold."
9. **Signification of Argala Source House:** Argala from 11th = gain/loss; consider the meaning of the house from which argala is caused, not just the target. Inimical argala planet harms; friendly helps. (Exception: 3rd house special argala.)
10. **BVB Short Method for Strength:** A simplified 3-source Graha Bala (Mooltrikonadi + Amsha + Kendradi) and 4-source Rashi Bala (Chara + Sthira + Dhristi + Graha) as alternative to full 5-source Jaimini calculation. Goel compares both and notes they sometimes give different Brahma → different dasha start → different results.
11. **Saturn Excluded from Brahma:** Saturn cannot be Brahma (but CAN be Maheswara). If Saturn qualifies by strength, pick the next stronger planet.
12. **Dual-Lordship Strength Hierarchy:** For Scorpio/Aquarius dasha years when Mars/Ketu (or Saturn/Rahu) are in different signs: stronger = more planetary associations → higher degrees → minutes → seconds.
13. **No Arudha Exceptions:** Goel explicitly does NOT apply the classical exception (lord in 1st/7th → 10th from house). Uses simple counting method throughout.
14. **Method-Comparison Validation:** For every major dasha, Goel presents multiple calculation methods (3 for Chara, 6 for Mandook, 3 for Navamsha, 3 for Trikon) and tests each against the same events. He explicitly identifies which method gives the "better explanation" — a pragmatic engineering approach.
15. **Maraca Identification Rule for Death:** Maracas = planets in 2nd/7th, lords of 2nd/7th, AND the signs in 2nd/7th (extended to Trimshamsha). Dasha at death relates to these in both birth and D30 charts.
16. **Varanasi Trimshamsha for Death Confirmation:** Specific D30 calculation (odd signs start from Aries, even from Libra, clockwise). When fatal conditions confirmed in D30 → death "after some problems"; confirmed in birth chart → "normal death without agony."
17. **Dwara/Bahya Rashi Examination (Atmanadi):** MD = dwara rashi (examine lord's associations/aspects); bahya rashi = sign as far from dwara as dwara is from lagna. Bad if malefic-aspected, malefics in Kendra, or DK involved.
18. **Trikona Jump is Essential:** Goel concludes the trikona jump (moving in trine groups) is what distinguishes Trikon dasha from Chara dasha — "if it were to be same as chara dasha then it will not be mentioned by sages."
19. **Female Chart Dasha Starts from 4th (IR method):** In Chara dasha Method 3 and others, female horoscopes start from 4th sign from lagna (prakriti chakra), reflecting gender-specific calculation.
20. **Amk Placement as Effort Indicator:** Amk in angles/trines/11th from AK = success with less effort; Amk in trik houses from AK = success after hard work. Also check in navamsha.
21. **Raj Yoga via BL/HL/GL Single-Aspect:** If one planet aspects bhava lagna, hora lagna, AND ghatika lagna → raj yoga (status, money, king-like life).
22. **Vaithanika Yoga:** Mars+Venus+Ketu in 3rd/11th from each other (mutual aspect) → success and fame.
23. **Navamsha Yoga Multiplication:** If number of yogas increases in navamsha → success with less effort. Important to check yogas in navamsha also.

## Conflicts & Ambiguities

- **Short vs. Full Jaimini strength method:** Goel explicitly notes the BVB short method and full Jaimini method sometimes yield different Brahma → different sthira dasha start → different results. For Charan Singh: Jaimini method gives Venus as Brahma (dasha from Sagittarius); short method gives Moon as Brahma. For Swami Vivekananda: Jaimini method gives Moon (dasha from Virgo); short method gives Mars. Goel does not definitively prefer one.
- **Number of karakas (7 vs 8):** Goel uses 7 but acknowledges other authors use 8 with Rahu. States 8 is unnecessary but does not fully resolve the dispute.
- **Arudha exceptions:** Goel acknowledges the strict longitudinal method exists and "needs further research," but uses simple counting without exceptions. This differs from other authorities.
- **Chara Dasha method divergence:** The three methods give different dasha periods and sometimes different dasha signs for the same date. Goel validates each against events and prefers the method that gives the "better explanation," but acknowledges all three can work.
- **Mandook Dasha applicability:** Method 1 requires 4+ planets in Kendras (excl. Rahu/Ketu); other methods have no condition. Goel tests Method 1 on charts without 4 Kendra planets and notes "this may not be a final conclusion."
- **Navamsha Dasha "adarsh sign":** Multiple interpretations exist; Goel tests three. The "lagna" referred to (birth vs. navamsha) is ambiguous in the sutras.
- **Sub-period calculation for several dashas:** Not specified by original authors (Mandook Method 2, Navamsha Method 3, Atmanadi, Brahma). Goel "logically deduces" sub-periods — acknowledges these are inferred, not authoritative.
- **P.S. Shastri's proportional dasha reduction (Chara Method 2):** Goel rejects the idea of reducing dasha by longitude proportion of lord, noting "Jaimini considers count from sign to sign" and "this is not supported by Parashar."
- **DK as "death giver":** Goel states Amk is "death giver to spouse and self" and DK is "giver of death" — the specific roles overlap and could cause confusion in death prediction.

## Extraction Notes

- **OCR quality issues:** The source is OCR'd with significant quality problems:
  - Page headers corrupted: "ChapterOl" / "Chapter0Ol" / "ter 01" instead of "Chapter 01"
  - Many numerical tables are garbled: dasha year columns scrambled, degree/minute values often corrupted (e.g., "9375]" for "93.75")
  - Chart diagrams lost — only planet position fragments remain (e.g., "Maz28:2" for Mars 28:2)
  - Words split/merged: "lagan" for "lagna", "Satum" for "Saturn", "Kartari" sometimes "Kartari" sometimes "Kartari"
  - Some rows in strength tables reduced to strings of digits and symbols
  - Sub-period tables often lose their sign labels, retaining only month counts
  - "Adarsh" vs "adarsh" inconsistent; "savya"/"apsavya" sometimes garbled
- **Gaps or unclear sections:**
  - The complete Graha Bala and Rashi Bala numerical tables (Ch 1, Ch 5, Ch 8) are largely unreadable; only fragmentary values could be extracted.
  - Many example chart diagrams (Indira Gandhi, Rajesh Khanna, etc.) are reduced to scattered planet-degree fragments; the full chart must be reconstructed from textual clues.
  - Some dasha period tables have lost their sign-to-period mapping; only the sequence of numbers remains.
  - The Trimshamsha calculation table (Ch 8) is partially garbled.
  - Method 5 (Labh Mandook) and Method 6 (trine-group Mandook) are described but not illustrated with example charts.
  - VKHL worked example (JFK Junior) has some numerical corruption in the final result.
- **Despite OCR issues:** The prose (rules, methods, example analysis) is largely intact and readable. The predictive logic, Goel's interpretive shortcuts, and the comparative-method approach are fully extractable.
