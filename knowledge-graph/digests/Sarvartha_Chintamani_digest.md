# Sarvartha Chintamani — Structured Digest

## Bibliographic Metadata
- **Title:** Sarvartha Chintamani
- **Author:** Vyankatesh Sharma (Venkatesh Sharma)
- **Translator:** J.N. Bhasin
- **Edition/Publisher:** Sagar Publications (Sagar's "Search-Light on Indian Astrology"); Publisher's note references Ranjan Publications, 16 Ansari Road, Darya Ganj, New Delhi
- **Approximate date of composition:** ~14th–15th century CE (post-BPHS, references Brihat Jataka & Yavanacharya; author describes himself as son of "Avvaya")
- **Source file:** extracted_markdown/Sarvartha_Chintamani.md
- **Total chapters/sections:** 17 chapters (Introductory Note + Chapters 1–17)
- **Scope:** A comprehensive bhava-centric (house-by-house) treatise on natal astrology. For each of the 12 houses it systematically treats the house, its lord, and its significator (kāraka), then gives detailed results for wealth, family, eyes, speech, brothers, mother, conveyances, sons, enemies, wife, longevity, father, career, gains, expenses, and sleep. It then covers Rāja yogas, longevity yogas (short/medium/long), cancellation of arishta (dangers to short life), and an exhaustive Vimshottari-style daśā-phala chapter for each planet (main periods and sub-periods). Notable for its ten-fold varga (divisional) scheme named Pārijāta → Vaiśeṣika and for defining "Gochar" as a strength condition rather than transit.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

### Chapter 1: Rashi Vichar (Definitions & Elementary Principles)
Foundational definitions: 12 signs and their lords, body parts, symbols, decantes (drekkāṇa), horas, navāṃśa and other vargas (1/7, 1/9, 1/10, 1/12, 1/16, 1/20, 1/24, 1/27, 1/40, 1/45, 1/60). Introduces the ten-fold division strength scheme named Pārijāta, Uttama, Gopura, Siṃhāsana, Pāravata, Devloka, Amara, Airāvata, Vaiśeṣika. Gives exaltation degrees, mūlatrikoṇa signs, planetary synonyms, traits, castes, directions, colours, benefic/malefic classification, Prāṇapada, Dhūma/Vyatipāta/Parivesha/Chapa/Ketu upagrahas, Vargottama, Veshi/Voshi/Ubhayachari, Upachaya houses (3,6,10,11), and the 9 avasthas of planets (Deepta etc.).

### Chapter 2: Lagna Phal (1st House Results)
Body health, complexion, head, gait, twins/animal births, delivery room, lamp at birth. The all-important rule (shloka 75) that the dispositor of a house-lord governs that house's traits. Fame from 1st+10th+Sun. Long-life yogas and short-life yogas tied to lagna lord strength.

### Chapter 3: Results of 2nd House
Family, dependents, mouth, speech, right eye, accumulated wealth, education, eating. Translator insists education is read from 2nd house (not 4th/5th). Wealth from brother/mother/son/enemy/wife/father/guru/self. Astrologer/maths/grammar/Vedanta yogas. Loss of wealth through government, thieves, fire.

### Chapter 4: Results of 3rd & 4th Houses
3rd: brothers (acquisition, loss, number), bravery, medicine, right ear, throat, food. 4th: mother, residence, relatives, conveyances, property, comforts, mind (deceitful/pure), drowning, bastard birth. Conveyance yogas with Venus/Jupiter. Mother's longevity calculation via rashmis.

### Chapter 5: Results of 5th & 6th Houses
5th: sons, pregnancy, childbirth, charities, food-feeding, belly diseases. 6th: diseases (eye, ear, leprosy, fever, consumption, urinary, asthma), enemies, imprisonment, limb-severance, sudden death. Diseases mapped to afflicting planet. Co-habitation type by 7th-house planet.

### Chapter 6: Considerations of 7th House
Wife, marriage, sex, pulses, curd, milk, coming/going, lost wealth. Multiple marriages, chaste vs bad wife, wife's status relative to native, wife's lagna determination, co-habitation with various women, breasts and private part descriptions, food enjoyed.

### Chapter 7: Considerations of 8th & 9th Houses
8th: longevity, death, rectum, mode of death, food, service, defeat. Death-mode yogas (hanging, poison, weapon, battle, sudden). 9th: Guru, luck, father, grandsons, compassion, tapas, income, hips, mind, family, charity, uncles. Father's troubles/longevity. Religion, charity, and Guru-devotion yogas.

### Chapter 8: Results of 10th, 11th & 12th Houses
10th: actions, ruling powers, fame, rain, public utility acts, honour, profession, knees, servants. Sanyas yogas (4 planets in kendra; Saturn aspecting lagna lord). 11th: income, feet, left ear, hair, elder coborns. 12th: expenses, hell, limb loss, left eye, feet, sleep. Deity worshipped (by 5th house planet).

### Chapter 9: Raj Yogas & Destruction of Good Yogas
Rāja yoga by exalted planets (5/6 planets), Vargottama lagna, Veshi yoga, kendra/trinal benefics, malefics in 3/6/11. Cancellation of debilitation (Neecha Bhanga) via kendra placement of the lord of the debilitation sign. Longevity divided into 3 age-khandas. Yogas for destruction of good results (Jupiter+Rahu, Sun+Moon in Leo, Saturn afflicting lagna+Moon).

### Chapter 10: Span of Life (Short Life Yogas)
Vipat/Pratyari/Vadha dasas. Gandanta births. Numerous short-life yogas by specific planetary combinations giving death in years 2,3,4,5,7,8,9,11,12,16,17,20,22,25,27,28,29,30,32. Moon in specific "maraka" degrees of each sign.

### Chapter 11: Cancellations of Yogas for Short Life (Arishta Bhanga)
Jupiter in lagna destroys all arishta. Strong benefic in kendra. Full Moon (farthest from Sun) in benefic navāṃśa. Rahu in 3rd/6th with benefic aspect. Moon in benefic varga even if ksheena. Retrogression and exaltation navāṃśa as protective.

### Chapter 12: Medium Longevity (and Long Life Yogas)
Longevity ranges 33–70 years with specific combinations, then 80, 86, 100, 108, 120 years, and yogas for thousands of years (rasāyana/mantra-based). Jupiter in kendra to lagna lord gives 70/100 years. Cancer lagna with Jupiter+Moon and benefics in kendras gives 120+ years.

### Chapter 13: Dasha Phala — Sun & Moon (9 Avasthas)
Defines 9 planetary avasthas (Deepta, Swastha, Mudita, Shanta, Heena, Dukhita, Vikala, Khala, Kopi) and their dasa results. Then detailed Sun dasa results by house/sign placement, and Moon dasa results by house/sign/strength.

### Chapter 14: Dasha Phala — Mars & Mercury
Mars dasa by exaltation, mūlatrikoṇa, own/enemy/friendly sign, navāṃśa, retrogression, strengths (Sthan/Dig/Kala/Nisarga), house placement. Mercury dasa similarly detailed.

### Chapter 15: Dasha Phala — Jupiter, Venus & Saturn
Jupiter, Venus, Saturn dasas by sign/dignity/house/strength/avastha. Jupiter as best benefic but can be turned malefic by heavy Saturn+Rahu affliction.

### Chapter 16: Dasha Phala — Rahu, Ketu & Sub-periods
Rahu exaltation in Taurus, Ketu in Scorpio. Rahu/Ketu dasa results by house. Sub-period results based on the position of the sub-lord relative to the main-lord (2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th, 7th, 8th, 9th, 10th, 11th, 12th from main lord). KEY: defines "Gochar" as a strength condition (own/exalted/friendly sign, not eclipsed, not in 6/8/12), NOT as transit. "Agochar/Yam-gochar" = weak/afflicted.

### Chapter 17: Miscellaneous
Nakshatra/tithi/lagna vriddhi-kshaya effects. Malefics in kendras giving bad results. Badhaka places (11th for movable lagna, 9th for fixed, 7th for common). Kāraka-sutras: detailed significators of each planet (Sun=father/health, Moon=mind/mother, Mars=bravery/enemies, Mercury=intellect/relatives, Jupiter=son/wealth/mantras, Venus=wife/enjoyment, Saturn=longevity/disease/poverty, Rahu=fame). Decantes named (Bird/Serpent/Nigad). Dry vs watery planets/signs.

## Key Principles (unique to or emphasized by this text)

1. **Dispositor Rule (shloka 2.75):** Whatever house (1st, 2nd, etc.) — if the lord of the sign occupied by the lord of that house is badly placed, the house suffers in its traits. The dispositor of a house-lord acts for the traits of that house. This is stated as "a very important rule and should never be lost sight of."
2. **Education from the 2nd house:** The author (and translator Bhasin) insist academic education be judged from the 2nd house, its lord, and Mercury — NOT from 4th or 5th as other authors do. The 4th relates to desires, 5th to intelligence/foresight.
3. **Ten-fold Varga Strength Scheme:** Pārijāta (2 divisions) → Uttama (3) → Gopura (4) → Siṃhāsana (5) → Pāravata (6) → Devloka (7) → Amara (8) → Airāvata (9) → Vaiśeṣika (10). Each named division gives escalating results (rich → emperor).
4. **9 Avasthas of Planets (Ch 13):** Deepta (exalted), Swastha (own sign), Mudita (friendly), Shanta (benefic sign), Heena (debilitated), Dukhita (inimical), Vikala (with malefic), Khala (defeated in war), Kopi (with Sun). Each gives distinct dasa results.
5. **"Gochar" = Strength, not Transit:** The text explicitly redefines Gochar (Ch 16, shlokas 3–4) as a planet in own/exalted/friendly sign, not eclipsed, not in 6/8/12 — i.e., a condition of excellence. "Agochar/Yam-gochar" = eclipsed, debilitated, in 6/8/12. This CONTRADICTS the usage in Gochar Phaladeepika where Gochar means transit.
6. **Bhavat Bhavam principle:** Extensively applied (e.g., 1st as 7th-from-7th for wife's longevity; 5th as 9th-from-9th for charity).
7. **Kharesh (22nd decante) & Mandi as Badhaka:** The lord of the 22nd decante and Mandi are highly obstructive significators.
8. **Rahu acts as Saturn; Ketu as Mars:** Translator's repeated note that Rahu always acts like Saturn (impotent, separative) and Ketu like Mars.
9. **Separative planets = Sun, Saturn, Rahu:** Used consistently for separation from family/residence/bed (12th house traits, 4th house, 2nd house).
10. **Lagna and Moon as dual lagnas:** Both must be strong for good longevity; affliction to both causes short life.

## Named Yogas

### Raja Yoga (generic)
- **Formation:** Planets in exaltation in kendras; 5–6 exalted planets; benefics in kendras/trines with malefics in 3/6/11; strong lagna lord.
- **Effects:** Kingship, affluence, fame.
- **Source:** Chapter 9, shlokas 1–38
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS Raja Yoga; agrees with Phaladeepika.

### Neecha Bhanga (Cancellation of Debilitation)
- **Formation:** A planet in debilitation, but the lord of that debilitation sign (OR the lord of its exaltation sign) is in a kendra from lagna.
- **Effects:** The native rules over all kings (rajādhirāja).
- **Source:** Chapter 9, shloka 13
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Semantically similar to BPHS Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga and Phaladeepika neecha bhanga. Sarvartha adds that debilitation in rashi but exaltation in navāṃśa also gives king-like fortune (shloka 9.15).

### Sanyas Yoga
- **Formation:** (a) 4 planets together in a kendra; OR (b) 4 planets together in non-kendra with their sign-lord in kendra/trine; OR (c) Saturn aspects the lagna lord and lagna lord aspects Saturn while lagna lord is weak.
- **Effects:** Renunciation (sanyas).
- **Source:** Chapter 8 (after 10th house), shlokas 1–4
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Mixed (spiritual)
- **Cross-text notes:** Supplements BPHS Sanyas yogas. Translator expands: needs Vairagya (Moon+4th afflicted by Saturn/Rahu) AND separation (2nd, 4th, 12th afflicted by separative planets Sun/Saturn/Rahu).

### Vargottama
- **Formation:** A planet in the same sign in both rashi and navāṃśa.
- **Effects:** Good results; strong planet.
- **Source:** Chapter 1, shloka 50
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS and Phaladeepika.

### Parijata / Gopura / Simhasana / Paravata / Devloka / Amara / Airavata / Vaisheshika Yogas (Varga-strength yogas)
- **Formation:** Count of beneficial vargas (divisions) in a planet's longitude: 2=Parijata, 3=Uttama, 4=Gopura, 5=Simhasana, 6=Paravata, 7=Devloka, 8=Amara, 9=Airavata, 10=Vaisheshika.
- **Effects:** Escalating wealth/status from "fairly rich" to "emperor."
- **Source:** Chapter 1, shlokas 23–31
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Unique scheme; supplements (does not contradict) BPHS varga scheme.

### Veshi / Voshi / Ubhayachari (Solar Yogas)
- **Formation:** Planets 2nd from Sun = Veshi; 12th from Sun = Voshi; both = Ubhayachari.
- **Effects:** Status, honour (especially benefic planets in Veshi).
- **Source:** Chapter 1, shloka 56; Chapter 9, shloka 6
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic if benefic planets involved
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS and Brihat Jataka.

### Adhi Yoga (referenced)
- **Formation:** Benefics in 6th/7th/8th from Moon.
- **Effects:** Referenced via translator notes (shloka 9.7 vicinity).
- **Source:** Chapter 9 (implied)
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS.

### Karaka Yoga
- **Formation:** Two or more planets in exaltation in kendras, mutually in kendras/trines from each other.
- **Effects:** Affluence and power.
- **Source:** Chapter 9, shlokas 6–7
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Unique name; conceptually related to BPHS Raja yoga.

### Gandanta Yoga
- **Formation:** Birth at junction of signs/nakshatras/tithis (specifically the end of one and start of another).
- **Effects:** Early danger to life; if survives, becomes protector of community with elephants/horses.
- **Source:** Chapter 10, shloka 17
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Malefic (but protective if survived)
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS and Phaladeepika on Gandanta.

### Arishta Bhanga (Cancellation of dangers to short life)
- **Formation:** Jupiter in lagna; OR strong benefic in kendra; OR full Moon in benefic navāṃśa; OR Rahu in 3rd/6th with benefic aspect.
- **Effects:** Destroys all dangers to short life.
- **Source:** Chapter 11, shlokas 1–22
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Supplements BPHS arishta bhanga principles.

### Bhavat Bhavam (principle, not a named yoga per se)
- **Application:** 1st as 7th-from-7th (wife's longevity); 5th as 9th-from-9th (charity); 3rd as 11th-from-5th (son's arms).
- **Source:** Throughout (e.g., shlokas 2.108, 3.64, 6.16)
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS and Phaladeepika.

## Dasha Systems

- **Vimshottari Dasha (implied):** The text treats dasas of Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, Ketu in the Vimshottari order. Chapters 13–16 give detailed results for each planet's main period (dasha) and sub-periods (bhukti/antardasha).
- **9 Avasthas determine Dasha Quality:** Deepta (exalted) → Swastha (own sign) → Mudita (friendly) → Shanta (benefic sign) → Heena (debilitated) → Dukhita (inimical) → Vikala (with malefic) → Khala (defeated) → Kopi (with Sun/combust). Each avastha gives specific results in the dasa.
- **Sub-period (Bhukti) Rules:** Results depend on the angular relationship of sub-lord to main-lord. 2nd/3rd from main = gentle results; 6th/8th/12th from main = harmful; 5th = sons; 7th = wife; 10th = career; 11th = gains. Benefic sub-lord in good dignity modifies even malefic main periods.
- **Gochar strength modifies results:** A "Gochar" (strong) planet gives good results even in difficult placements; "Agochar" gives bad results.
- **Vipat/Pratyari/Vadha:** 3rd/5th/7th dasa from the birth dasa (counted) give physical suffering (Ch 10, shloka 14). Specifically: 3rd=Vipat, 5th=Pratyari, 7th=Vadha.

## Transit (Gochar) Rules

- **CRITICAL DIFFERENCE:** This text uses the term "Gochar" NOT in the sense of planetary transit (as in Gochar Phaladeepika) but as a **condition of planetary strength/excellence**. Definition (Ch 16, shlokas 3–4):
  - **Gochar planet** = located in own sign, exaltation, friendly sign; not eclipsed by Sun; not in 6th/8th/12th house.
  - **Agochar / Yam-Gochar planet** = eclipsed, debilitated, in sign other than mūlatrikoṇa/friendly, located in 6th/8th/12th.
- **No transit-from-Moon or transit-from-Janma-Rashi rules** are given in this text. The text is firmly a natal (janma) and dasha-phala text, not a gochar/transit text.
- **Translator's note (Ch 14, shloka 17):** Explicitly states "The word Gochar used in the shloka has not been used in any sense of transit."
- This is a **contradicts** edge with Gochar Phaladeepika (Pulippani), which uses Gochar exclusively as transit.

## Ashtakavarga

- **Not addressed.** The text does not cover Ashtakavarga principles. The varga scheme here refers to divisional charts (navāṃśa, drekkāṇa, etc.), not the Ashtakavarga bindhu system.

## Bhava (House) Results

This is the text's core specialty — comprehensive bhava analysis for all 12 houses:

- **1st (Lagna):** Body, health, complexion, head, gait, fame, longevity. Lagna lord strength is paramount. Lagna+10th+Sun = fame.
- **2nd:** Family, dependents, mouth, speech, right eye, accumulated wealth, **education** (unique), eating. Wealth from various relations. Loss of wealth via government/thieves/fire.
- **3rd:** Brothers (acquisition/loss/number via rashmis), bravery, medicine, right ear, throat, food, sources of danger (serpents, poison).
- **4th:** Mother (longevity calculation), residence, relatives, conveyances (Venus/Jupiter), property, comforts, mind (deceitful/pure), drowning, bastard birth, sexual perversity.
- **5th:** Sons, pregnancy, childbirth (male/female/twins/impotent), charities, food-feeding, belly diseases, mantra/spiritual initiation, deity worshipped.
- **6th:** Diseases (mapped to afflicting planet — Sun=heat, Moon=water, Mars=blood, Mercury=skin/nerves, Jupiter=phlegm, Venus=loose motions, Saturn=wind/colic, Rahu/Ketu=spirits), enemies, imprisonment, limb-severance, sudden death, urinary troubles.
- **7th:** Wife (character, status, number, chastity), marriage timing, sex, co-habitation types, wife's lagna determination, breasts/private part descriptions, food enjoyed, urinary diseases.
- **8th:** Longevity, death, mode of death (hanging/poison/weapon/battle/sudden), rectum, defeat. Death-mode from 8th lord's decante (Pasha/Sarpa).
- **9th:** Guru, luck, father (troubles/longevity), grandsons, compassion, tapas, income, religion, charity, Guru-devotion, foreign lands. Father-son relationship yogas.
- **10th:** Actions, ruling powers, fame, rain, public utility acts (temple renovation, tank digging), honour, profession, knees, servants. Sanyas yogas. Cruel/gentle orders from 10th lord.
- **11th:** Income (directional sources), feet, left ear, hair, elder coborns (longevity/loss). Income from direction of 11th sign/lord.
- **12th:** Expenses (legitimate/illegitimate), hell after death (next-world determination via Sun/Moon decante lord), limb loss, left eye, feet, sleep (bed quality), foreign residence.

## Muhurta / Electional

- **Not addressed as a primary subject.** However, the text notes (Ch 1, shloka 5) that all natal principles apply equally to horary (Prashna). It mentions Raudra Muhurta (last half before sunrise) and Brahma Muhurta in the context of short-life yogas (Ch 10, shloka 23). It references Nishek Lagna (conception ascendant) for birth predictions (Ch 2, shlokas 38–42).

  - Badhaka by lagna type (movable/fixed/common)
  - Karaka-sutras (Ch 17) with elaborate significations

## Conflicts & Ambiguities

- **"Gochar" semantics:** This text says Gochar = a strength condition (own/exalted/friendly sign, not in 6/8/12). Gochar Phaladeepika (Pulippani) and modern usage says Gochar = transit. **Direct contradiction.** A reader must interpret "gochar" in Sarvartha Chintamani contextually, not as transit.
- **Education house:** Sarvartha (2nd house) vs BPHS/Phaladeepika (4th/5th house). Translator Bhasin is emphatic that 2nd is correct.
- **Exaltation of Rahu/Ketu:** Sarvartha (Ch 16) gives Rahu exaltation in Taurus, Ketu in Scorpio. Some other texts (e.g., certain commentaries) give Rahu exaltation in Gemini/Virgo. Minor variation.
- **Retrograde exalted planet:** Translator notes debate whether a retrograde exalted planet acts as debilitated; Bhasin rejects this view but notes Jupiter in exaltation+retrogression can still be turned malefic by heavy Saturn/Rahu affliction.
- **Shloka numbering and OCR:** Several shloka numbers are misprinted or out of sequence (e.g., Ch 3 jumps); some verses (Ch 9, shloka 7) are complex and the "Karaka yoga" definition is unclear.
- **Meaning of "Kharesh":** The 22nd decante (12th from lagna decante-wise) — its lord is highly obstructive. This is a specialized term not universally defined.
- **Sub-period shloka for Mars in 10th house:** Translator notes the benefic sub-period shloka is missing from the text (Ch 16, after Mars dasa).

## Extraction Notes

- **OCR quality issues:** The OCR is heavily garbled in Sanskrit portions (Devanagari) and in tabular divisional-chart data (Ch 1, pages 9–27). Many varga tables are unreadable. English translation text is generally legible but contains frequent OCR artifacts: "0111" for "benefic," "Vilagna" for "lagna," "149185" for "benefic," "rasha" for "rashi," broken character sequences in Devangari.
- **Gaps or unclear sections:**
  - The 1/60th division (Shashtiamsha) names list (shlokas 1.15–1.20) is partially unreadable in the table.
  - Shlokas 1.33–1.35 (Pranpada calculation) are acknowledged by translator as "not quite clear."
  - Some shloka numbers are duplicated or misnumbered (e.g., two shloka "13" sequences in Ch 5).
  - The sub-period results for Mars in 10th house (Ch 16) are explicitly missing per translator.
- **Translator's voice:** Bhasin inserts extensive notes with real-life horoscope examples (Nehru, Sanjay Gandhi, Indira Gandhi, Hitler, Queen Wilhelmina, Swami Shivananda, Shri Chaitanya). These notes are valuable but represent Bhasin's interpretation, not always the original text.
- **Completeness:** The text covers all 12 bhavas, Raja yogas, 3 tiers of longevity, arishta bhanga, and full dasha-phala for all 9 planets. It is natal + dasha focused; it does NOT cover Ashtakavarga, muhurta (electional), or transit (gochar in the modern sense).
- **Chapter count:** The TOC lists 18 items but the "Miscellaneous" chapter (Ch 17) is the final content chapter; the 18th item ("Miscellaneous" pages 367–371) appears to be the same as Ch 17. Effectively 17 chapters of content.
