# Predict Effectively Through Yogini Dasha (V.P. Goel) — Structured Digest

## Bibliographic Metadata

- **Author:** V.P. Goel
- **Guided by:** K.N. Rao
- **Publisher:** Vani Publications (Hindu Astrology Series), F-291 Saraswati Kunj, 25 IP Extn., Delhi-92

- **Total chapters:** 14 (plus an Annexure on presiding deities and direction lords of Yoginis)
- **Scope:** This is a research monograph that solves the "riddle" of Yogini Dasha — namely that its 36-year cycle repeats mechanically two, three, or four times in a lifetime. Goel's breakthrough is a *progression* method (inspired by Jaimini's Chara Dasha) in which the nakshatra contributing the running Yogini becomes the Progressed Lagna, and the Vimshottari nakshatra lord (not the Yogini lord) is used as the predictive key. The book specifies the 8 Yoginis, the nakshatra-to-Yogini mapping, sub-period computation, a per-Yogini prediction manual, divisional-chart application, a double-transit timing technique, and three blind-tested classroom cases.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

### Chapter 1: Classical
BPHS and Mansagri prescribe eight Yoginis with individual lords and dasha periods of 1 to 8 years (total 36). The starting Yogini at birth is derived from the Moon's nakshatra: nakshatra number from Ashwini + 3, divided by 8, the remainder giving the Yogini Sr.No. Balance of dasha at birth is proportional to the Moon's remaining longitude in the nakshatra. Sub-periods are proportional to each Yogini's major period, beginning with the major dasha lord.

### Chapter 2: Research
This is the heart of Goel's contribution. Because a 36-year cycle repeats, the second/third cycle cannot be read identically to the first. Goel's solution: (a) the nakshatra contributing the running Yogini becomes the *Progressed Lagna* (planets stay as in natal); (b) 24 nakshatras fit 3 cycles while Ashwini, Bharani and Krittika are *clubbed* with Poorva Bhadrapada, Uttara Bhadrapada and Revati respectively (since they share the same navamsha signs and yield the same Yogini). Nakshatras thus progress in zodiacal order across cycles, mirroring Jaimini's Chara Dasha.

### Chapter 3: Formula
Lays down the analysis method. Write each dasha as `Yogini/Nakshatra` (e.g. Sankata/Mrigshira). Note the nakshatra (star) lord and delineate through three reference points: (1) nakshatra lord in birth chart, (2) nakshatra lord in progress chart, (3) relation of the dasha lord from the nakshatra lord in the progress chart. For Sankata, Ketu is also considered. Rahu/Ketu as nakshatra lord act as their dispositor and conjunct planet. The sub-period of the nakshatra lord generally fulfils the promise; any planet influenced by both dasha and nakshatra lords is potent; any planet influencing both overrides the period. The 11th house is the house of expansion.

### Chapter 4: Mangala
Mangala (Moon, 1 year) operates through Shravana, Chitra and Ardra. Example charts show love affair (EC 26), house construction (EC 2), daughter's birth (EC 9), promotion (EC 4), political appointment (EC 5), lost elections (EC 10), marriage (EC 5 in Ardra), political exposure (EC 6), and jail during Emergency (EC 13). For dwipada nakshatras (Chitra spans Virgo/Libra) the Progressed Lagna changes mid-period.

### Chapter 5: Pingala
Pingala (Sun, 2 years) operates through Punarvasu, Dhanishta and Swati. Examples: career start (EC 5), marriage (EC 7, EC 10), Home Minister appointment (EC 13), near-fatal accident (EC 1), Chief Minister (EC 8), father's death (EC 9), mother's death (EC 8). Punarvasu and Dhanishta are dwipada, splitting the Progressed Lagna.

### Chapter 6: Dhanya

Examples: Chief Election Commissioner (EC 5), son's birth (EC 11), marriage (EC 12, EC 24), husband's death (EC 6), coronation of Queen Elizabeth (EC 14), daughter's birth and higher study (EC 1), ministerial appointment (EC 15), marriage (EC 16). Pushya is singled out as generating events with lifelong effect.

Examples: husband's assassination and becoming PM (EC 17), serious illness and death (EC 18), job change (EC 1), Empress of India (EC 14), father's death (EC 3), MP election (EC 10), third PM term (EC 17), daughter's birth and law partnership (EC 7), husband unseated (EC 7), PM of India (EC 6), sickly birth (EC 30, EC 31).

Magha and Bharani involve Ketu/Venus as star lords.

Examples: marriage (EC 11, EC 4), assassination (EC 6), resignation and death (EC 15), marriage and affair (EC 19), Chief Election Commissioner (EC 17), Class I service (EC 1), death (EC 5), marriage (EC 9). ### Chapter 12: Divisional Charts
Yogini Dasha is "micro Vimshottari" and applies equally to divisional charts. The star lord of the running Yogini is examined in the corresponding divisional chart (the Progressed Lagna becomes the divisional chart Lagna). Worked examples: Dreshkana (D3) for Indira Gandhi's courage and comeback; Chaturthamsha (D4) for house purchase; Saptamsha (D7) for children; Dashamsha (D10) for profession; Navamsha (D9) for marriage; Dwadamsha (D12) for parents' death.

### Chapter 13: Transit
Quoting BPHS 3.6, Goel applies transit to two progressed reference points: the Progressed Lagna and the placement of the star lord in the progress chart. Double transit of Saturn and Jupiter is required: Saturn influences the house/lord within 1 year of the event; Jupiter within 6 months; Sun within 1 month (nakshatra lord included); Moon within 3 days. A 7-step pinpoints timing: ascertain house/lord → verify promise → fix sub-period → Saturn transit → Jupiter transit → Sun transit (month) → Moon transit (day).

## Yogini Dasha System — Complete Specification

### Computation Method
- **Starting point:** Count the Moon's nakshatra from Ashwini (Ashwini = 1). Add 3 to this number. Divide the sum by 8. Ignore the quotient; the remainder (1–8, with 0 taken as 8) is the Sr.No. of the operating Yogini at birth. Abhijit nakshatra is not counted.
- **Cycle order (Sr.No. order):** Mangala → Pingala → Dhanya → Bhramari → Bhadrika → Ulka → Siddha → Sankata, then repeats.
- **Total cycle duration:** 36 years (1+2+3+4+5+6+7+8).
- **Each Yogini duration:** Mangala 1, Pingala 2, Dhanya 3, Bhramari 4, Bhadrika 5, Ulka 6, Siddha 7, Sankata 8 years.
- **Balance of dasha at birth:** Proportional to the Moon's longitude remaining in the birth nakshatra. (Remaining degrees / 13°20′) × full dasha period of the starting Yogini.
- **Progression across cycles (Goel's method):** After the first 36-year cycle, the nakshatra contributing each Yogini advances by 8 positions in zodiacal order (skipping Ashwini, Bharani, Krittika which are clubbed). The nakshatra now contributing the running Yogini defines the *Progressed Lagna* (the sign containing that nakshatra), while all planets retain their natal positions. This is the key to non-mechanical interpretation of the 2nd/3rd/4th cycles.

### Antardasha (Sub-periods)
- Each major period is divided into 8 sub-periods in the order Mangala, Pingala, Dhanya, Bhramari, Bhadrika, Ulka, Siddha, Sankata — **beginning with the major dasha lord itself**.
- The duration of each sub-period is proportional to that sub-period Yogini's own major period.
- **Formula (Goel):** *Sub period (in days) = (Dasha years of major lord Q) × (Dasha years of sub lord P) × 10.*
- Example: Siddha sub-period in Ulka major = 6 × 7 × 10 = 420 days = 1 year 2 months. The same product gives the sub-period of Ulka in Siddha major (symmetry).
- **Dwipada/Tripada rule:** When the nakshatra contributing the Yogini spans two signs, the Progressed Lagna changes mid-period. The change occurs within the sub-period proportional to the nakshatra's longitude in each sign. The book's Table V marks the exact sub-period of Lagna change for each Yogini (e.g., Mangla/Chitra changes from Virgo to Libra in Ulka sub; Sankata/Mrigshira changes from Taurus to Gemini after Bhramari sub).

## Prediction Rules

1. **Yogini/Nakshatra notation:** Always write the running dasha as `Yogini/Nakshatra` (e.g. Sankata/Mrigshira). The nakshatra — not the Yogini — drives the prediction.
2. **Progressed Lagna:** The sign containing the nakshatra contributing the running Yogini becomes the Lagna for the duration of that dasha. All planets keep their natal positions. For dwipada/tripada nakshatras the Lagna changes mid-period (rule of three).
3. **Three reference points:** Judge (a) the nakshatra lord in the *birth* chart, (b) the nakshatra lord in the *progress* chart, and (c) the relation of the dasha lord from the nakshatra lord in the progress chart.
4. **Nakshatra lord > Yogini lord:** Results are delineated with reference to the nakshatra (star) lord, using Vimshottari nakshatra lords. This is the key to the analysis.
5. **Sankata special rule:** For Sankata dasha, Ketu is also considered alongside Rahu.
6. **Rahu/Ketu as star lord:** When Rahu or Ketu is the nakshatra lord, they act as (and are read as) their dispositor and any planet conjunct with them.
7. **Fulfilment sub-period:** The sub-period of the nakshatra lord within any major dasha generally fulfils the promise of that dasha.
8. **Dual-influence planet:** Any planet influenced by *both* dasha lord and nakshatra lord is potent to deliver the promise in its own sub-period.
9. **Override planet:** Any planet *influencing* both dasha lord and nakshatra lord overrides the indications of that period.
10. **Eleventh house of expansion:** The 11th lord and any planet in the 11th are important. Watch for the birth 11th becoming Progressed Lagna, or the birth Lagna falling in the progress 11th — these periods are significant.
11. **Birth Lagna in progress chart:** The house placement of the birth Lagna in the progress chart indicates the *type* of result expected (e.g. 8th = suddenness/tragedy, 6th = sickness/obstacles, 7th = marriage/partnership, 10th = career).
12. **Progress cannot exceed birth:** The progress chart cannot supersede the promise of the birth chart — only time and manifest it.
13. **Double transit (Saturn + Jupiter):** For an event to fructify, Saturn in transit must aspect/conjunct the relevant house or lord (from Progressed Lagna or star lord) within 1 year; Jupiter within 6 months; Sun within 1 month (include the nakshatra lord); Moon within 3 days.
14. **7-step timing:** (i) Fix house/lord from Progressed Lagna and star lord; (ii) verify promise in birth & progress; (iii) fix sub-period; (iv) Saturn transit; (v) Jupiter transit; (vi) Sun transit (month); (vii) Moon transit (day).
15. **Divisional confirmation:** The star lord is examined in the corresponding divisional chart (D3 courage, D4 property, D7 children, D9 spouse, D10 profession, D12 parents). Make the Progressed Lagna the divisional Lagna.

## Yogini-specific Results

### Mangala Yogini
- **General effects:** Short 1-year period of Moon significations — mother, mind, emotions, travel, 4th-house matters, love affairs, fluctuation.
- **Benefic placement:** Moon well-placed and connected to 10th/11th gives promotion, political appointment, new ventures, marriage (when connected to 7th).
- **Malefic placement:** Moon in 6/8/12, with Rahu, aspected by malefics gives love affair without marriage, loss of election, confinement, mental anguish.
- **Nakshatra progression:** Shravana (Capricorn), Chitra (Virgo→Libra in Ulka sub), Ardra (Gemini, Rahu acts as its dispositor/conjunct).

### Pingala Yogini
- **General effects:** 2-year period of Sun significations — father, authority, career, government, health, ego.
- **Benefic placement:** Sun connected to 10th/11th gives career start, chief ministership, home minister appointment.
- **Malefic placement:** Sun afflicted gives father's death, near-fatal accidents, mother's death (when Mars is maraka for mother).
- **Nakshatra progression:** Punarvasu (Gemini→Cancer in Sankata sub), Dhanishta (Capricorn→Aquarius), Swati (Libra, Rahu acts as dispositor).

### Dhanya Yogini
- **General effects:** 3-year period of Jupiter significations — wisdom, wealth, children, marriage, dharmic rise. Pushya period specifically generates events with lifelong effect.
- **Benefic placement:** Jupiter yogakaraka aspecting 10th gives CEC appointment, crowning (Queen Elizabeth), marriage, son's birth.
- **Malefic placement:** Jupiter afflicted or as 7th/8th lord gives husband's death, maraka events.
- **Nakshatra progression:** Vishakha (Libra→Scorpio in Sankata sub), Pushya (Cancer), Shatbhishaj (Aquarius, Rahu acts as dispositor).

### Bhramari Yogini
- **General effects:** 4-year period of Mars significations — energy, conflict, surgery, assassination, property, abrupt events. Often brings Maraka periods.
- **Benefic placement:** Mars aspecting 10th gives PM appointment, MP election, third PM term.
- **Malefic placement:** Mars as maraka gives husband's assassination, serious illness, death, arrest.
- **Nakshatra progression:** P.Bhadrapad (Aquarius→Pisces in Sankata sub, +Ashwini clubbed), Anuradha (Scorpio), Ashlesha (Cancer).

### Bhadrika Yogini
- **General effects:** 5-year period of Mercury significations — intellect, communication, business, education, elections, litigation, partnership.
- **Benefic placement:** Mercury strong gives re-election to International Court, Bharat Ratna, marriage, father's promotion.
- **Malefic placement:** Mercury as maraka gives death (plane crash), assassination, arrest, ashram scandal.
- **Nakshatra progression:** Magha (Leo, Ketu acts as Mars), Bharani (Taurus, Venus), U.Bhadrapad (Pisces, Saturn), Jyeshtha (Scorpio, Mercury).

### Ulka Yogini
- **General effects:** 6-year period of Saturn significations — long struggle, sorrow, endurance, death, structural change, loss of position, foreign stays.
- **Benefic placement:** Saturn exalted aspecting 10th gives coronation (King George VI), President election, food ministership.
- **Malefic placement:** Saturn as maraka gives death (self, mother, wife, son), lost kingdom, election loss, foreign hardship.
- **Nakshatra progression:** Moola (Sagittarius, Ketu acts as Mercury), P.Phalguni (Leo, Venus), Krittika (Aries→Taurus in Siddha sub), Revati (Pisces, Mercury).

### Siddha Yogini
- **General effects:** 7-year period of Venus significations — marriage, comfort, vehicles, women, political power, arts, sensory pleasure.
- **Benefic placement:** Venus connected to 10th/11th gives PM ship, President election, marriage, Bharat Ratna, engineering admission.
- **Malefic placement:** Venus afflicted or as maraka gives death (human bomb), child's death, husband's affair.
- **Nakshatra progression:** Rohini (Taurus, Moon), P.Ashadha (Sagittarius, Venus), U.Phalguni (Leo→Virgo in Sankata sub).

### Sankata Yogini
- **General effects:** 8-year period — the longest and most turbulent. Rahu/Ketu significations: sudden, karmic, hidden events, death, intrigue, foreign, obsession, endings.
- **Benefic placement:** Rahu/Ketu acting as benefic gives marriage, Chief Election Commissionership, Class I service.
- **Malefic placement:** Rahu/Ketu as maraka gives assassination (Indira Gandhi), resignation and death, marital tragedy (wife's suicide).
- **Special rule:** Ketu is considered in addition to Rahu throughout Sankata.
- **Nakshatra progression:** Hasta (Virgo, Moon), Mrigshira (Taurus→Gemini after Bhramari sub), U.Ashadha (Sagittarius→Capricorn).

## Relationship to Vimshottari Dasha
- Goel explicitly calls Yogini Dasha **"micro Vimshottari"** — one 36-year cycle is roughly one-third of Vimshottari's 120-year cycle; all 27 nakshatras together contribute 123 years of Yogini (3+ cycles), making the two systems structurally parallel.
- The dasha lord in Yogini is **different from the nakshatra lord**, whereas in Vimshottari they are the same. Goel uses the *Vimshottari nakshatra lords* as the predictive key inside Yogini — "progressing the Yogini periods with the Vimshottari nakshatra lords is the key to the analysis of Yogini."
- Yogini's maximum single-period is 8 years vs Vimshottari's 20 years, so Yogini is better at narrowing down timing.
- Yogini is robust to slight birth-time errors because small shifts in the Moon's degree change the balance of dasha but not the starting Yogini, so events can still be timed with remarkable closeness.
- Both systems have universal Kaliyug application per the sages (Goel cites Parashara).
- Divisional-chart analysis and double-transit rules are applied identically; Goel reuses the Vimshottari nakshatra lord as the "star lord" inside Yogini.

## Cross-Text Relations
- **Parallels BPHS Vimshottari on:** nakshatra-based computation, proportional dasha balance at birth, proportional sub-periods, universal Kaliyug applicability, double transit of Saturn/Jupiter, divisional-chart analysis, BPHS 3.6 cited directly for transit.
- **Differs from Vimshottari on:** total cycle length (36 vs 120 years), dasha lord ≠ nakshatra lord, 8 Yoginis vs 9 planets, 3 nakshatras per dasha lord vs Vimshottari's 3 (but with different lords), need for a progression method to handle repeated cycles.
- **Unique to Yogini Dasha:** (a) the (NakshatraNo + 3) ÷ 8 computation; (b) clubbing of Ashwini/Bharani/Krittika with P.Bhadrapad/U.Bhadrapad/Revati; (c) the Progressed Lagna concept tied to the running nakshatra; (d) three-cycle nakshatra progression mirroring Jaimini Chara Dasha; (e) explicit treatment of dwipada/tripada nakshatras causing mid-period Lagna change; (f) Ketu additionally considered throughout Sankata; (g) Yogini Dasha Chakra (cyclic diagram) and presiding deities/direction lords (Annexure).

## Conflicts & Ambiguities
- **OCR damage to Chapter 1 Table I:** The classical table of Yogini lords is partially garbled; durations and lords were cross-verified against K.N. Rao's foreword (Mangala/Moon, Pingala/Sun, Dhanya/Jupiter, Bhramari/Mars, Bhadrika/Mercury, Ulka/Saturn, Siddha/Venus, Sankata/Rahu-Ketu) and against every per-Yogini chapter.
- **Durations of Yoginis (1–8 vs other traditions):** Some classical traditions assign different durations; Goel strictly follows 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 years in sequence. No conflict within this text.
- **Sub-period formula symmetry:** Goel's formula `Q_years × P_years × 10` is symmetric in P and Q, which (as he notes) gives the same number of days for "P in Q" and "Q in P" — this is a feature, not a conflict, but should not be confused with Vimshottari's asymmetric sub-periods.
- **Clubbing ambiguity:** Goel clubs Ashwini↔P.Bhadrapad, Bharani↔U.Bhadrapad, Krittika↔Revati based on shared navamsha signs. The text does not specify what to do if a native is actually born in Ashwini/Bharani/Krittika — the implication (from the foreword and examples) is that the *clubbed* nakshatra is used for progression, while the birth Yogini is still computed from the actual birth nakshatra (both yield the same Yogini anyway).
- **Annexure (deities/direction lords):** OCR too damaged to extract the presiding deities and direction lords reliably; only Bhadrika→Vaishnavi/Mahendri is partially legible.
- **Goel's "second-cycle start from next nakshatra" vs K.N. Rao's three options:** Rao's foreword lists three possible solutions (start from Lagna/Sun; Chara-Dasha-style zodiacal progression; repeat order but shift start nakshatra). Goel adopts a variant of Rao's third option (zodiacal progression with clubbing), which is internally consistent but is Goel's own research choice, not the only classical answer.
