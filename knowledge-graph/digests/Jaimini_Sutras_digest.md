# Jaimini Sutras — Structured Digest

## Bibliographic Metadata
- **Title:** Jaimini Sutras (Jaiminisutras / Sri Jaiminisutras)
- **Author:** Sage Jaimini (disciple of Vedavyasa; also author of Poorva Mimamsa Sutras and Jaimini Bharata)
- **Translator/Commentator:** Prof. B. Suryanarain Rao, B.A., M.R.A.S., F.R.H.S. — revised and edited by his grandson B. V. Raman, D.Sc., F.R.A.S. (Editor, *The Astrological Magazine*)
- **Approximate date of composition:** ~5,000+ years before present (per Prof. Rao's preface); Jaimini was a contemporary of Vedavyasa. Third (revised) edition of this translation published 1949 (Salivahana Saka 1871).
- **Source file:** extracted_markdown/Jaimini_Sutras.md (~8,988 lines, OCR'd with `## Page N` headers)
- **Total Adhyayas/Padas translated:** 2 Adhyayas × 4 Padas = 8 sections (the translator notes the work may originally extend to 4 or 8 Adhyayas, but only these 2 Adhyayas were available and translated; he calls them "complete in themselves"). The task spec's expectation of "4 × 4 = 16" does not match the source text actually present.
- **Scope:** This is the foundational treatise of **Jaimini astrology**, a parallel system to Parashari (BPHS) astrology. Its defining departures from Parashari are: (1) sign-based (Rasi) Dashas rather than planet/Nakshatra-based Dashas like Vimshottari; (2) dynamic Chara Karakas (planet with highest degrees becomes Atmakaraka, etc.) instead of fixed Naisargika Karakas; (3) Rasi Drishti (sign-to-sign aspects) with movable/fixed/common rules different from planetary drishti; (4) Argala (planetary intervention/obstruction) — a concept absent in BPHS; (5) Pada/Aruḍha Lagna and Upapada as primary predictive reference points; (6) special death-inflicting planets Rudra, Maheswara, Brahma used with Shoola Dasas for longevity. The sutras use the **Katapayadi** mnemonic system (Ka, Ta, Pa, Ya) to encode house numbers inside Sanskrit words (e.g. "Dara" = 82 reversed = 28 ÷ 12 = 4, hence "Dara" means the 4th house in Jaimini — NOT the 7th as in Parashari). Constellations (Nakshatras) are barely mentioned (only Sutra 2.4.18 refers out to Udu Dasas from other works). Predictions flow from Karakamsa (Navamsa of Atmakaraka), Pada Lagna, Upapada, and various special Lagnas (Hora, Ghatika, Varnada, Bhava, Chandra).

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary (Adhyaya + Pada)

### Adhyaya 1, Pada 1 — Foundations: Aspects, Karakas, Argala, Pada, Rasi Dasa
(35 sutras, Pages 18–55) Opens with "Upadesam Vyakhyasyamah" — "I shall now explain." Defines Rasi Drishti (movable↔fixed except next sign; common signs aspect each other). Defines Argala: 4th, 2nd, 11th from a body are Argalas; 3rd from malefics gives Papa-argala; 10th, 12th, 3rd obstruct Argala; 5th/9th (Trikona) counteract; for Ketu, counting is reversed. Defines the 7 Chara Karakas (Atma, Amatya, Bhratri, Matri, Putra, Gnatika, Dara) — planet with highest degrees = Atmakaraka, descending order for the rest; Rahu fills vacancies when two planets merge. Defines Rasi Dasa (years = number of signs from Rasi to its lord; +1 for exaltation, −1 for debilitation; 12 years if lord in own house). Defines Arudha/Pada Lagna. Defines Varnada, Hora, Ghatika, Bhava and Chandra Lagnas. Notes Vrischika has two lords (Kuja + Ketu) and Kumbha has two lords (Rahu + Sani).

### Adhyaya 1, Pada 2 — Karakamsa Results
(121 sutras, Pages 56–103) Defines **Karakamsa** = the Navamsa occupied by Atmakaraka; planets conjunct/aspecting Karakamsa determine profession, character, diseases, spouse, religion, Moksha. Explains Gulikakala (time ruled by Gulika, son of Sani) computation. Gives results of each planet in Karakamsa (Sun→politics, Moon+Venus→wealth, Mars→metals/fire-works, Mercury→commerce/weaving, Jupiter→Vedic learning, Venus→government/women, Saturn→fame in own line, Rahu→weapons/thievery, Ketu→elephants). Lists diseases from planetary positions in 4th/5th from Karakamsa (leprosy, consumption, etc.). Defines Jaimini-version **Kemadruma Yoga**: malefics in 2nd and 8th from Lagna/Pada/Arudha → great poverty.

### Adhyaya 1, Pada 3 — Pada Lagna Results; Royalty & Poverty Yogas
(44 sutras, Pages 103–122) Results of planets in 11th, 12th, 2nd, 7th from Pada/Arudha Lagna. Benefics in 11th from Pada → wealth from lawful means; malefics → ill-gotten gains. Malefics in 12th → extravagant spending on sinful deeds. Special Raja Yogas: one planet aspecting all of Lagna, Hora Lagna, Ghatika Lagna → ruler; aspecting 5 of 6 special Lagnas → ordinary Raja Yoga. Grahasamya (equal planet counts in pairs of houses like 2nd/12th, 6th/9th, 12th/6th, 4th/10th) → imprisonment. Mentions Chandra+Guru or Chandra+Sukra in 4th from Atmakaraka → royal paraphernalia.

### Adhyaya 1, Pada 4 — Upapada; Spouse, Children, Brothers, Diseases
(49 sutras, Pages 122–140) Defines **Upapada** = Pada/Arudha of the 12th from Lagna (alternative: Pada of 7th from Lagna). 2nd from Upapada → wife's fate; malefic there → wife dies or native takes Sanyasa. Exalted planet in 2nd from Upapada → many wives. Various planetary combinations in 2nd from Upapada indicate wife's diseases (blood, bones, fever, corpulence, nasal, ear, teeth, limbs). Sani+Rahu in Karakamsa → notorious rake. Two planets in Atmakarakamsa → community leader. Religious tendencies determined from planet next to Amatyakaraka (Bhratrikaraka) — benefic→worship of devatas, malefic→evil spirits. Nigraha (subjugation) and Anugraha (blessing) forms of Mantra use mentioned; 33 crores of Devatas framework explained.

### Adhyaya 2, Pada 1 — Longevity Determination; Rudra, Maheswara, Brahma
(57 sutras, Pages 140–168) Longevity computed from lords of 1st and 8th in Chara/Sthira/Dwiswabhava signs. Three systems: (1) Lagna + 8th lord, (2) Lagna + Chandra Lagna, (3) Lagna + Hora Lagna. Alpayu 0–32 yrs, Madhyayu 33–66 yrs, Purnayu 67–100 yrs. **Samvada** (majority reconciliation) — if systems disagree, Lagna+Hora Lagna wins. **Kakshya Hrasa** (reduction) by Sani; **Kakshya Vriddhi** (increase) by Guru. Defines Dwara Rasi (sign commencing Dasa) and Dwarabahya/Bhoga Rasi (sign at same count from Dwara). Defines three special death planets: **Rudra** (stronger of 8th lords from Lagna and 7th), **Maheswara** (lord of 8th from Atmakaraka, with special rules for Rahu/Ketu involvement), **Brahma** (strongest of 6/8/12 lords from stronger of Lagna/7th, in odd sign among Parswa Rasis; if Sani/Rahu/Ketu becomes Brahma, take 6th planet from him). Death occurs in Shoola Dasas (1, 5, 9) from these planets.

### Adhyaya 2, Pada 2 — Death of Parents, Relatives; Diseases of Death
(32 sutras, Pages 168–181) Marakas for father: stronger of Sun and Venus. Marakas for mother: stronger of Moon and Mars. Benefic aspects on these → death in Shoola Dasa from them; malefic aspects → painful death. Cause of death from planet in 3rd from Lagna/Karaka: Sun→royal displeasure, Moon→consumption, Mars→wounds/fire/weapons, Saturn→windy complaints, Saturn+Gulika→poison/snakes/chains/water, Ketu→contagious/watery diseases, Jupiter→dropsy/melancholy, Venus→venereal. Saturn+Rahu or Saturn+Ketu in 1st/12th conjunctions → native fails to perform parents' death ceremonies.

### Adhyaya 2, Pada 3 — Sthira, Navamsa, Prana Dasas; Strength of Rasis
(28 sutras, Pages 181–192) **Sthira Dasa**: 7 years for movable, 8 for fixed, 9 for common signs; commences from Rasi occupied by Brahma. **Navamsa Dasa**: 9 years each, commences from Lagna if odd, from Abhimukha Rasi (8th/6th/7th from Lagna for movable/fixed/common respectively) if even. Strength of Rasis (in priority order): (1) conjunction with Atmakaraka; (2) larger number of planets; (3) exaltation/friendly/Moolatrikona; (4) Naisarga (permanent) — movable < fixed < common; (5) lord's strength. Death of father in Shoola Dasa from 9th of stronger Lagna/7th; of mother from 4th; of brothers from 3rd; of children/sisters from 5th; of elder brothers from 11th; of paternal uncles from 9th. Malefic aspects/conjunctions are a source of strength for Rasis (Jaimini's distinctive rule).

### Adhyaya 2, Pada 4 — Paka/Bhoga Rasis; Manduka, Yogardha, Drig, Trikona Dasas
(37 sutras, Pages 192–209) **Paka Rasi** (Dwara) = sign commencing Dasa; **Bhoga Rasi** (Bahya) = sign at same count from Dwara as Dwara is from Lagna. Malefics in Paka/Bhoga → imprisonment/disease. Defines **Manduka Dasa** ("frog-jump") — Dasas jump Kendra→Panapara→Aupoklima, and Chara→Sthira→Dwiswabhava. **Yogardha Dasa** = half of Chara+Sthira combined, begins from stronger of Lagna/7th. **Drigdasa** = commences from 9th from Lagna, follows Thrikonarupa Pada pattern (2nd, 5th-from-2nd, 4th, 5th-from-4th...). **Trikona Dasa** = commences from strongest of 1/5/9. Final sutra (2.4.37 "Siddhamanyat") — Jaimini says all principles explained here are truths propounded in other works and hold good in all calculations. Text ends; appendix is a biography of Prof. B. Suryanarain Rao.

## Key Principles (unique to Jaimini)
1. **Chara Karaka system:** Karakatwas (significatorships) are *dynamic* — assigned by planet's longitude, not fixed. The planet with the highest degrees in the chart becomes Atmakaraka; next highest becomes Amatyakaraka; and so on. This is the polar opposite of BPHS's Naisargika (fixed) Karakas.
2. **Rasi Drishti:** Signs aspect each other (movable↔fixed except adjacent; common↔common). Planetary aspects are secondary.
3. **Argala & Virodhana:** 4th, 2nd, 11th from a body create Argala (intervention); 10th, 12th, 3rd create Virodhana (obstruction); 5th/9th counteract. For Ketu, reverse the counting.
4. **Rasi-based Dashas:** Vimshottari (planet/Nakshatra based) is not used. Chara Dasha, Sthira Dasha, Navamsa Dasha, Shoola Dasha, Manduka Dasha, Yogardha Dasha, Drigdasa, Trikona Dasha are all sign-based.
5. **Pada / Aruḍha Lagna:** Derived point = count from Lagna to its lord, then same count from lord. Predictions about wealth, status, spouse flow from Pada and Upapada.
6. **Karakamsa:** Navamsa occupied by Atmakaraka — treated as an alternate Lagna for predicting profession, character, spouse, religion, Moksha.
7. **Katapayadi mnemonic:** Sanskrit words encode house numbers (Ka, Ta, Pa, Ya = 1; reversed reading; mod 12). "Dara" = 4 (not 7), "Bhagya" = 2, "Sula" = 11, "Kama" = 3, "Ripha" = 10, "Neecha" = 12, etc.
8. **Savya/Apasavya counting:** Odd signs count zodiacally (left-to-right); even signs count reverse (right-to-left). Exceptions for Vrishabha, Vrischika, Kumbha, Simha.
9. **Two-lord signs:** Vrischika is ruled by both Kuja and Ketu; Kumbha is ruled by both Sani and Rahu.
10. **Rudra / Maheswara / Brahma:** Special death-inflicting planets used with Shoola Dasas to time death — a framework absent in BPHS.
11. **Dwara/Bhoga (Paka) Rasis:** Sign commencing Dasa and its "mirror" sign used to predict imprisonment, disease, death.
12. **Grahasamya:** Equal planet counts in paired houses (2/12, 6/9, 12/6, 4/10) cause imprisonment.
13. **Six Lagnas for Raja Yoga:** Lagna, Chandra Lagna, Navamsa Lagna, Drekkana Lagna, Hora Lagna, Ghatika Lagna — one planet aspecting all six (or five) → Raja Yoga.

## The Eight Karakas
| Karaka | Role | Significator (Jaimini rule) |
|--------|------|------------------------------|
| Atmakaraka | Self / Soul | Planet with the highest number of degrees among the 7 planets (Sun→Saturn) or 8 (incl. Rahu) |
| Amatyakaraka | Career / Minister / Advisor | Planet next in degrees to Atmakaraka |
| Bhratrikaraka | Brothers / Siblings | Planet next in degrees to Amatyakaraka |
| Matrikaraka | Mother | Planet next in degrees to Bhratrikaraka |
| Putrakaraka | Children | Planet next in degrees to Matrikaraka |
| Gnatikaraka | Cousins / Obstacles | Planet next in degrees to Putrakaraka |
| Darakaraka | Spouse (wife) | Planet with fewer degrees than Gnatikaraka |
| Kalatrakaraka | (8th — sometimes conflated with Dara) | When two planets tie in degrees and merge, Rahu fills the vacancy (Su. 1.1.11 note). Some Acharyas (Su. 1.1.19) combine Matri and Putra into one, freeing a slot. The Contents page (Page 3) lists "Kalatra-karaka or Lord of wife" alongside Atmakaraka as if distinct from Darakaraka, but the sutras themselves use Dara for spouse. |

Note: When two or three planets obtain equal degrees, they merge into one Karaka; the vacancy is filled by Rahu (counted in reverse order). If Rahu also ties, take the Naisargika (permanent) Karaka for that event (Su. 1.1.11 notes).

## Chara Dasha System
- **Computation:** Years for a Rasi Dasa = number of signs counted from that Rasi to the sign where its lord is placed. E.g. Mesha lord Kuja in Simha (5th from Mesha) → Mesha Dasa = 5 years.
- **Duration:** Variable per sign (1–12 years). If lord is in own house or in 12th from the Rasi → 12 years. Each Rasi Dasa is divided by 12 to get Antardasas (sub-periods). In some later systems (Adhyaya 2 Pada 4), each Rasi Dasa = 9 years uniformly.
- **Order:** For odd signs, count zodiacally (Savya / right); for even signs, count reverse (Apasavya / left). Exceptions: Vrishabha and Vrischika count zodiacally (despite being even); Kumbha and Simha count reverse (despite Simha being odd / Kumbha even).
- **Special rule for two-lord signs (Vrischika, Kumbha):** If both lords are in the Rasi itself → 12 years. If only one is present → 0 years from that one (use the other). If neither is present → take the stronger of the two and compute from him. Strength preference: lord in own house > lord conjunct other planets > lord giving more years > exalted lord.
- **Modifications:** Exalted lord → +1 year; debilitated lord → −1 year.
- **Results:** Predictions made by examining the sign, its lord, planets in it, aspects (Rasi Drishti), Argalas, and Karakamsa relative to the Dasa Rasi. Paka Rasi (Dwara) and Bhoga Rasi (Bahya) govern the period's fruits — malefics there → imprisonment/disease; benefics → prosperity.

## Argala (Intervention)
- **Definition:** A planetary "bolt" or "obstruction" — a planet in 4th, 2nd, or 11th from any Bhava/planet/significator modifies (helps or hinders) the flow of influences from that body. Argala literally means "bolt" or "bar".
- **Types:**
  - Primary Argala (Su. 1.1.5): 4th, 2nd, 11th houses from the body — "Dara" (4), "Bhagya" (2), "Sula" (11) by Katapayadi.
  - Papa-Argala (Su. 1.1.6): Malefics (more than two) in 3rd ("Kama"=3) from the body produce evil Argala.
  - Trikona Argala (Su. 1.1.9): 5th and 9th from the body similarly influence Argala.
- **Obstruction (Virodhana):** Planets in 10th ("Ripha"=10), 12th ("Neecha"=12), and 3rd ("Kama"=3) from Argala obstruct it (Su. 1.1.7).
- **Survival rule:** If obstructing planets are fewer or weaker than Argala-causing planets, Argala survives (Su. 1.1.8).
- **Ketu exception (Su. 1.1.10):** For Ketu, calculate Argala and its obstruction in reverse order (7th from Ketu is Argala; 9th from Ketu is Argala; 5th from Ketu is obstruction).
- **Two varieties:** Shubha Argala (beneficial intervention) and Papa Argala (malefic intervention).
- **Effects:** Argala modulates the expression of any Bhava — e.g. Shubha Argala on Paka/Bhoga Rasis → wealth; Papa Argala → ordinary wealth or poverty.

## Rasi Drishti (Sign Aspects)
- **Rules (Su. 1.1.2–4):**
  - Movable (Chara) signs — Mesha, Kataka, Thula, Makara — aspect all Fixed (Sthira) signs *except* the one next to them.
    - E.g. Mesha aspects Simha, Vrischika, Kumbha (not Vrishabha).
  - Fixed (Sthira) signs — Vrishabha, Simha, Vrischika, Kumbha — aspect all Movable signs *except* the one next to them.
    - E.g. Vrishabha aspects Kataka, Thula, Makara (not Mesha).
  - Double-bodied / Common (Dwiswabhava) signs — Mithuna, Kanya, Dhanus, Meena — aspect each other (all four mutual).
  - Planets occupying those signs also aspect accordingly (Su. 1.1.4).
- **Difference from planetary drishti (BPHS):** BPHS uses *planetary* aspects (all planets 7th; Mars special 4th/8th; Jupiter 5th/9th; Saturn 3rd/10th). Jaimini uses *sign-based* aspects where the sign itself aspects other signs based on movable/fixed/common category — independent of which planet occupies it. Both systems coexist in Jaimini predictions.

## Pada / Aruḍha
- **Computation (Su. 1.1.29–31):** Count signs from Lagna to its lord (= N). Count N signs from the lord's position → that sign is Pada Lagna (Arudha).
  - Special case 1 (Su. 1.1.30 "Swasthe daraha"): If lord is in 4th from Lagna, the 7th from the lord becomes Pada Lagna.
  - Special case 2 (Su. 1.1.31 "Sutastejanma"): If lord is in 7th from Lagna, Lagna itself becomes Pada Lagna (since 7th from 7th = Lagna). Jaimini's rule: when the count would return to the original sign, the same sign is taken (the "7th-from-7th" exception).
- **Upapada (Su. 1.4.1):** Take the 12th from Lagna; find its Pada/Arudha → Upapada. (Alternative commentator view: take the 7th from Lagna and find its Pada.) Used for marriage, spouse, sexual relations, children (5th from Upapada), brothers (3rd/11th from Upapada).
- **Karakamsha (Su. 1.2.1):** The Navamsa occupied by Atmakaraka. Treated as a Lagna — planets in 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 7th, 9th, 10th, 12th from Karakamsa give specific results (profession, speech, courage, houses, children, spouse, religion, career, Moksha).
- **Other Pada-derived Lagnas:** Hora Lagna (birth-ghatis ÷ 2.5 ghatis, counted from Sun's sign); Ghatika Lagna (ghatis mod 12, from Sun's sign); Varnada Lagna (computed from Lagna + Hora Lagna with odd/even sign rules); Bhava Lagna (= ordinary Lagna); Chandra Lagna (= Moon sign / Janma Rasi).

## Named Yogas (Jaimini-specific)

### Kemadruma Yoga (Jaimini version)
- **Formation:** Malefics in 2nd and 8th houses from Janma Lagna or Arudha Lagna (also applies to 3rd from Karakamsa). Chandra's aspect on these malefics intensifies the poverty.
- **Effects:** Great poverty, abject misery.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 2, Sutra 119–120.
- **Contrast with BPHS:** BPHS defines Kemadruma as no planets on either side of Moon (2nd/12th from Moon empty). Jaimini's version is broader — malefics in 2nd/8th from Lagna/Pada.

### Raja Yoga (Jaimini)
- **Formation:** (a) One planet aspects all six Lagnas — Lagna, Hora Lagna, Ghatika Lagna, Chandra Lagna, Navamsa Lagna, Drekkana Lagna (Su. 1.3.24–25). (b) One planet aspects five of the six → ordinary Raja Yoga (Su. 1.3.26). (c) Benefics in 2nd, 4th, 5th, 8th from Lagna or 7th lord (Su. 1.3.32). (d) Benefics in 2nd and 4th equal to Karaka, or 5th and 8th equal (Su. 1.3.30). (e) Exalted planet in Arudha Lagna with Chandra, Guru, Sukra and no evil Argala (Su. 1.3.26 notes).
- **Effects:** Royalty, rulership, high political position.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 3, Sutras 24–44.

### Grahasamya Bandha Yoga (Imprisonment Yoga)
- **Formation:** Equal number of planets in paired houses — 2nd & 12th, 6th & 9th, 12th & 6th, or 4th & 10th — from Lagna. Combinations for evil (Papa-yoga) → certain imprisonment; for good (Shubha-yoga) → trials but acquittal.
- **Effects:** Chains, beating, imprisonment, government displeasure.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 3, Sutra 42.

### Daridra Yoga (Poverty)
- **Formation:** Evil planets in beneficial signs and benefics in evil signs (Su. 1.3.34). Or Arudha Lagna falling in Dusthana (6/8/12) from Lagna instead of Kendra/Trikona (Su. 1.3.19).
- **Effects:** Poverty, wretchedness.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 3.

### Moksha Yoga
- **Formation:** Ketu in 12th from Karakamsa; or Karakamsa in Mesha/Dhanus with benefics; or benefic in 12th from Karakamsa (Su. 1.2.68–71). Karakamsa in Mesha or Dhanus with Ketu in 12th → highest Moksha.
- **Effects:** Final emancipation, residence in superior Lokas.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 2, Sutras 68–71.

### Bahudara Yoga (Many Wives)
- **Formation:** Exalted planet in 2nd from Upapada, or 2nd from Upapada in exalted Navamsa (Su. 1.4.6).
- **Effects:** Many wives.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 4.

### Bahuputra Yoga (Many Children)
- **Formation:** 5th from 7th of Upapada, its lord, Kalatra Karakamsa, and its lord — all aspected by Sun, Rahu, and Jupiter (Su. 1.4.25).
- **Effects:** Many children.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 4.

### Anapatya Yoga (Childlessness)
- **Formation:** 7th from Upapada, its lord, Kalatra Karakamsa, and its lord — all aspected or conjoined by Mercury, Saturn, and Venus (Su. 1.4.24).
- **Effects:** No issues.
- **Source:** Adhyaya 1, Pada 4.

## Cross-Text Relations (vs Parashari BPHS)
- **Parallels BPHS on:**
  - Use of 9 planets (Sun→Saturn + Rahu/Ketu) and 12 signs; lords of signs mostly align.
  - Concept of Karakas (significators) — but Jaimini's are *dynamic* (Chara) vs BPHS's *fixed* (Naisargika).
  - Pada / Aruḍha Lagna (BPHS Chapter on Arudha also defines it the same way).
  - Kemadruma Yoga (both texts mention it, but with different formation rules).
  - Raja Yoga concept (angular-trinal lord relation) — Jaimini adds his own multi-Lagna version.
  - Special Lagnas — Hora, Ghatika, Varnada find echoes in BPHS.
  - Longevity classification (Alpa/Madhya/Purna) — same three tiers, but computed differently.
- **Differs from BPHS on:**
  - **Dasha system:** Jaimini uses *sign-based* Dashas (Chara, Sthira, Navamsa, Shoola, Manduka, Yogardha, Drig, Trikona) — NOT Vimshottari or other Nakshatra/planet-based Dashas. Constellations are barely mentioned.
  - **Aspects:** Jaimini uses *Rasi Drishti* (sign-to-sign, based on movable/fixed/common) — BPHS uses *planetary drishti* (7th for all, plus special aspects for Mars/Jupiter/Saturn).
  - **Karakas:** Jaimini's Chara Karakas (Atma, Amatya, etc.) are dynamic by degrees — BPHS's Naisargika Karakas are fixed (Sun=father, Moon=mother, Mars=brother, etc.).
  - **Sign lordships:** Jaimini gives Vrischika two lords (Mars + Ketu) and Kumbha two lords (Saturn + Rahu); BPHS gives each sign one lord only.
  - **Maraka for father/mother:** Jaimini says stronger of Sun/Venus kills father, stronger of Moon/Mars kills mother (Su. 2.2.1–2). BPHS uses 2nd/7th from the relevant Karaka.
  - **Death-timing planets:** Jaimini uses Rudra, Maheswara, Brahma + Shoola Dasas — BPHS uses 2nd/7th Maraka planets and Dashas.
  - **Mnemonic encoding:** Jaimini uses Katapayadi to encode house numbers inside Sanskrit words ("Dara"=4, "Bhagya"=2) — BPHS uses direct terminology.
  - **Strength of Rasis:** Jaimini explicitly states malefic conjunction/aspect is *a source of strength* for a Rasi (Su. 2.3.27) — opposite of general Parashari view.
- **Unique to Jaimini (absent in BPHS):**
  - Chara Karaka system (8 dynamic Karakas).
  - Rasi Drishti (sign aspects).
  - Argala & Virodhana.
  - Pada Lagna and Upapada as central predictive reference.
  - Karakamsa (Navamsa of Atmakaraka) as a Lagna.
  - Rudra, Maheswara, Brahma planets.
  - Shoola, Manduka, Yogardha, Drig, Trikona Dasas.
  - Dwara / Dwarabahya (Paka / Bhoga) Rasi framework.
  - Grahasamya (equal planet count) for imprisonment.
  - Six-Lagna Raja Yoga (Lagna, Hora, Ghatika, Chandra, Navamsa, Drekkana).
  - Varnada Lagna computation.
  - Gulikakala used with Karakamsa for poison/snake-bite predictions.

## Conflicts & Ambiguities
- **Missing Adhyayas:** Translator notes Jaiminisutras may originally extend to 4 or 8 Adhyayas; only Adhyayas 1 and 2 were available and translated. Adhyayas 3 and 4 (if they exist) remain undiscovered.
- **8th Karaka ambiguity:** Contents page lists "Kalatra-karaka" as if distinct from Darakaraka, but the sutras only enumerate 7 Karakas (Atma, Amatya, Bhratri, Matri, Putra, Gnati, Dara) and use Rahu to fill vacancies when planets tie. Some Acharyas (Su. 1.1.19) combine Matri + Putra into one Karaka, which would create a vacancy — suggesting the 8th slot is fluid.
- **Upapada definition (Su. 1.4.1):** Two commentator views — (a) Pada of 12th from Lagna; (b) Pada of 7th from Lagna. Prof. Rao presents both and leaves the choice to the reader's judgment and experience.
- **Savya/Apasavya exceptions (Su. 1.1.27 "Nakwachit"):** General rule is odd→zodiacal, even→reverse, but the Sutra cryptically says "in some places this does not apply." Commentators identify Vrishabha, Vrischika, Kumbha, and Simha as exceptions, but the rationale is unclear.
- **Sani's Kakshya Hrasa (Su. 2.1.10–13):** General rule says Sani reduces the longevity category, but Sutras 11–13 carve out exceptions (some say no reduction; no reduction if Sani is in own/exalted house; no reduction if Sani has only malefic aspects). The conditions are inconsistent across commentators.
- **Drekkana lords (Page 71–72):** Varahamihira says 1st/5th/9th lords; others say 1st/12th/11th; yet others say different rules for movable vs fixed/common. Prof. Rao admits he cannot decide which is correct.
- **Brahma for Sani/Rahu/Ketu (Su. 2.1.48):** If Sani, Rahu, or Ketu becomes Brahma, take the 6th planet from him instead. The "6th planet" rule and its rationale are not fully transparent.
- **Strength from malefics (Su. 2.3.27):** Malefic aspects/conjunctions are said to be a *source of strength* for Rasis — opposite of the general rule. Prof. Rao defers to commentators.
- **Hora Lagna calculation:** Prof. Rao's examples use 2.5 ghatis per Hora but the arithmetic in the notes is inconsistent (e.g. "14½ ghatis ÷ 2½ = 5 with remainder 2 ghatis" is arithmetically off). Likely OCR or compositor errors.
- **OCR quality:** The text is heavily garbled in places — Sanskrit Devanagari is mis-OCR'd (e.g. "ApuvAYA 1, ८4०4 4" for "Adhyaya 1, Pada 4"), English words are fractured ("Fubhas" / "shubhas"), and numerical Katapayadi calculations sometimes show wrong figures. Cross-verification with a printed edition is advisable for any precise calculation.

## Extraction Notes
- **OCR quality issues:**
  - Devanagari sutras frequently mangled (e.g. "उप्र. 1, Papa 3" for "Adhyaya 1, Pada 3"; "Avuvava 2, Papa 4" for "Adhyaya 2, Pada 4").
  - English running text sometimes fractured mid-word ("asti/ onary / ekly" — line breaks inside words).
  - Katapayadi arithmetic occasionally shows impossible figures (e.g. "60 divided by 12 there will be no balance, but the author asks the readers to take always the last balance and when 60 is divided by 12 it goes four times (48) and the last balance is 12" — mathematically 60÷12=5 exactly, no remainder; the "last balance is 12" is a commentator convention for exact multiples).
  - Page 70–73 region has duplicated content (Gulikakala explanation appears twice).
  - Sutra numbering occasionally inconsistent (e.g. Su. 1.2.16 and Su. 1.2.18 both labeled "18" in places; Sutras 1.3.15–16 both labeled "16").
- **Gaps or unclear sections:**
  - The original Sanskrit sutras are quoted in Devanagari but often garbled; the transliteration (e.g. "Su. 1.— Upadesam Vyakhyasyamah") is more reliable than the Devanagari.
  - Sutra 2.1.18 is described by Prof. Rao as "very tough and even the commentators have not come to our rescue."
  - The Drigdasa (Su. 2.4.21) and Trikona Dasa (Su. 2.4.24) rules are terse; Prof. Rao's notes are tentative ("I am very diffident about this explanation").
  - Adhyayas 3 and 4 are entirely absent — the text ends at Adhyaya 2, Pada 4, Sutra 37 with "Siddhamanyat" ("the rest is established elsewhere"), followed by the biography of Prof. Rao.
- **Coverage:** Despite OCR issues, all major Jaimini concepts (8 Karakas, Rasi Drishti, Argala, Pada/Upapada, Karakamsha, Chara Dasha, Rudra/Maheswara/Brahma, Shoola/Sthira/Navamsa/Manduka/Yogardha/Drig/Trikona Dashas, longevity determination, death-timing) are extractable with high confidence.
