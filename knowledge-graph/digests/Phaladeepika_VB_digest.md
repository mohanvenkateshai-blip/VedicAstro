# Phaladeepika (Viswa Bharati Edition) — Structured Digest

## Bibliographic Metadata
- **Title:** Phaladeepika
- **Author:** Mantreswara (family deity: Sukuntalamba; born in Tinnevelly/Tirunelveli district; flourished ~16th century CE)
- **Translation:** Viswa Bharati / Santiniketan edition — translated by Panditabhushana V. Subrahmanya Sastri, B.A. (Asst. Secretary to the Govt. of Mysore, Retd.; translator of Brihat Jataka, Jatakaparijata, Sripatipaddhati)
- **Publisher:** Aruna Press, Bangalore (first edition, 6th January 1937)
- **Source file:** extracted_markdown/Phaladeepika_English_Translation.md
- **Total chapters:** 28 Adhyayas (the text itself states "27 chapters" + Upasamhara in Ch.28; the colophon of Ch.28 calls it the 28th)
- **Scope:** A comprehensive treatise on Phalita Jyotisha (predictive astrology) covering definitions, planetary natures, zodiacal divisions, Shadbalas, profession, yogas, Rajayogas, bhava effects, sign-as-lagna effects, marriage, female horoscopy, children, longevity, diseases/death, bhava analysis, exit from world, planetary conjunctions, Vimshottari Dasha, Bhukti/Antardasa, Kalachakra Dasha, Ashtakavarga, Upagrahas, transits (Gochara), ascetic yogas, and a concluding chapter.
- **Relationship to Kapoor edition:** The VB (Sastri) translation is the earliest English rendering (1937) and is notably more literal/technical, preserving Sanskrit terminology throughout with parenthetical transliterations. It includes translator's notes citing Varahamihira, Parasara, and cross-references to Sastri's own Jatakaparijata edition. The Kapoor edition is a later, more interpretive rendering. Both share the same 28-chapter structure and Sanskrit source text, but the VB edition is more philologically conservative and includes alternative readings (e.g., for Sloka III.16 and the XXV Adhyaya). The VB edition's treatment of transits (Ch.26) and Ashtakavarga (Ch.23-24) is particularly detailed with Vedha rules and the 8-division transit-timing method.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

### Chapter 1: Samjna Adhyaya (Definitions)
Defines the 12 bhavas and their multiple names, the Kalapurusha body correspondences, sign abodes, sign lords, exaltation/debilitation signs and degrees, Moolatrikona signs and portions, Prishtodaya/Ubhayodaya/Sirshodaya signs, diurnal/nocturnal signs, Urdhva/Adhah/Sama/Vakra signs, Chara/Sthira/Ubhaya signs, Dwara/Bahis/Garbha, Dhatu/Mula/Jeeva, Krura/Saumya, odd/even, male/female signs. Defines Kendra, Panaphara, Apoklima, Chaturasra, Upachaya, Trikona, Dusstthana, Subhastthana, Leenastthana.

### Chapter 2: Graha-bheda (Planets and their Varieties)
Significations of each planet (Sun through Saturn, Rahu, Ketu), physical descriptions and temperaments, places/regions governed, occupations/beasts/birds, natural friendships and enmities, temporal (Tatkalika) friendships, planetary periods, castes, Satva/Rajas/Tamas gunas, seasons, parent-pairs for day/night births, relations signified, body/soul and five senses, benefic/malefic classification, genders (male/female/eunuch), presiding deities, elements, grains, countries, precious stones, metals, apparel, flavours, bodily marks, ages, tree-types. Notes on Rahu and Ketu characteristics.

### Chapter 3: Rasi-bheda (Divisions of the Zodiac)
Defines the Dasa Vargas (10 divisions: Rasi, Drekkana, Navamsa, Dasamsa, Hora, Trimsamsa, Saptamsa, Shashtyamsa, Kalamsa, Dwadasamsa), Saptavargas, Shadvargas, Vargottamamsa. The five planetary Avasthas (infancy through extremis). Hora, Drekkana, Dwadasamsa, Trimsamsa, Navamsa lords. Shashtyamsa (Krura/Saumya). Vaiseshikamsas (Parijata, Uttama, Gopura, Simhasana, Paravata, Devaloka, Suraloka, Airavata) from 2-9 Varga combinations. Effects of planets weak/strong in the 10 Vargas. Ayudha/Pasa/Nigala/Pakshi decanates. Method to find Lagna from Mandi or Moon.

### Chapter 4: Shadbala Nirupana (Determination of the Six Strengths)
Defines the six-fold strength: Kalaja (temporal), Cheshta (motional), Uchchaja (exaltation-derived), Dik (directional), Ayana (declinational), Sthana (positional). Night-strong planets (Mars, Moon, Venus); Mercury strong always. Digbala assignments (Sun/Mars in 10th, Venus/Moon in 4th, Mercury/Jupiter in 1st, Saturn in 7th). Nisargabala ordering (Saturn weakest → Sun strongest). Retrograde planets strong. Rahu/Ketu strength positions. Positional strength (exaltation=1 Rupa, Moolatrikona, own, friendly, inimical, debilitation=nil). Kendradi bala. Importance of 7th aspect. Natural friendship preferable to temporal. Jupiter strongest in warding off evil. Chandrakriya, Chandravasttha, Chandravela (60 effects). Pakshabala. Shadbala thresholds: Sun 6.5, Moon 6, Mars 5, Mercury 7, Jupiter 6.5, Venus 5, Saturn 5 Rupas. Bhavabala computation.

### Chapter 5: Karma-vibhaga (Profession and Livelihood)
Sources of wealth and profession from the 10th house, its lord, planets in the 10th, and the Navamsa lord of the 10th. Acquisition of wealth without exertion. Country/time of acquisition.

### Chapter 6: Yoga-bhava (Yogas)
The Pancha Mahapurusha yogas (Ruchaka, Bhadra, Hamsa, Malavya, Shasha) from Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn in own/exaltation in Kendra. Chandra-based yogas (Sunapha, Anapha, Durudhara, Kemadruma). Surya-based yogas (Subhavesi, Subhavasi, Subhobhayachari and their Papa counterparts). Subhakartari/Papakartari, Susubha. Mahabhagya, Kesari, Sakata, Adhama/Sama/Varishtha. Vasumati, Amala, Pushkala. Subhamala, Asubhamala, Lakshmi, Gauri. Saraswati, Srikantha, Srinatha, Virinchi. Parivartana yogas (66 total: 30 Dainya, 8 Khala, 28 Maha). Kahala, Parvata. Raja Yoga and Sankha Yoga. Seven Sankhya yogas (Vallaki/Veena, Dama, Pasa, Kedara, Sula, Yuga, Gola). Adhiyoga. 12 auspicious yogas (Chamara, Dhenu, Saurya, Jaladhi, Chhatra, Astra, Kama, Asura, Bhagya, Khyati, Suparijata, Musala). 12 inauspicious yogas (Avayoga, Nisswa, Mriti, Kuhu, Pamara, Harsha, Dushkriti, Sarala, Nirbhagya, Duryoga, Daridra, Vimala). Another Duryoga from dusthana lords strong in Kendra/Trikona.

### Chapter 7: Maharaja Yogas (Great Royal Yogas)
Numerous Rajayoga combinations from planets in exaltation/Swakshetra in Kendras, Digbala, Vargottama, Moon conditions, Venus-Jupiter combinations, Mars in specific Lagnas, mutual exchanges of 9th and 10th lords. **Neecha Bhanga Rajayoga** (sl.26-30): explicitly enumerates FIVE types of cancellation of debilitation giving kingship. This is a key chapter for cross-translation reconciliation.

### Chapter 8: Effects of Planets in the 12 Bhavas
Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, Ketu — each in the 12 houses from Lagna. Rahu acts like Saturn; Ketu like Mars. Planets in the centre of a bhava give full effects.

### Chapter 9: Effects of Signs as Lagna
Effects of Mesha through Meena happening to be the Lagna. Same effects from the Moon's sign. Effects of planets in Uchcha, Swakshetra, Mitrakshetra, Satrukshetra, Neecha, Astangata, Vakra, Vargottama Navamsa.

### Chapter 10: Kalatra-bhava (The 7th House / Marriage)
Yogas for acquisition/loss of wife, chaste/fortunate wife, separation, liaison with others' wives, wifelessness/childlessness, multiple wives, predicting number of wives, loss of wife/husband, happy couple, remarriage, death of both spouses. Finding wife's Janmarasi, direction, time of marriage.

### Chapter 11: Stri Jataka (Female Horoscopy)
Effects in a female's horoscope from the 7 bhavas. Character, nature, luck of a female. Husband's appearance/status, mutual relationship. Alpasutarkshas, diseased organs, unchastity, fortunate/chaste female, barrenness. Effects of female birth in Trimsamsas of each sign. Effects of birth in specific asterisms. Saubhagya yoga. Menstruation, impregnation timing.

### Chapter 12: Santana (Children)
Yogas for acquisition of children, childlessness, late children, children through second wife, many daughters, family extinction, adoption, loss of children, many children, all-male children. Time of conception, determining number of issues, fecundity strength, remedies for childlessness, time of son acquisition, delivery time, prediction bases.

### Chapter 13: Ayur-bhava (Longevity)
Longevity first to be ascertained. Opinions on correct Lagna time. Children under 12 cannot be calculated. Remedies for Balarishta. Balarishta, Yogarishta, Alpayus, Madhyamayus, Purnayus defined. Maximum human life and three divisions. Dinamrityu and other child-death conditions.

### Chapter 14: Roga-marana-purvaja-bhavishya-janma (Disease, Death, Past/Future Births)
Diseases from planets in houses, death-inflicting planets, past and future births inferred from planetary positions at death.

### Chapter 15: Bhava-chintana (Method of Studying Bhava Effects)
How to study effects of the 12 bhavas — the bhava itself, its lord, its Karaka, aspects, conjunctions. General principles for predicting bhava effects.

### Chapter 16: Dvadasa-bhava Samudaya-phala (General Effects of 12 Bhavas)
Detailed effects of each of the 12 bhavas, their lords, planets in them, and aspects.

### Chapter 17: Niryana-bhava (Exit from the World)
Signs of approaching death, timing of death, the death-inflicting planet, Niryana bhava analysis.

### Chapter 18: Digraha-yoga (Conjunctions of Two Planets)
Effects of all pairwise planetary conjunctions (Sun+Moon, Sun+Mars, etc.) in the 12 houses.

### Chapter 19: Dasha-phala (Dasas and their Effects)
Vimshottari Dasha system. Dasha years of each planet. Effects of planets in various positions during their Dasas. Maraka planets. Kendradhipati dosha principle (Jupiter/Venus owning Kendras cause evil). Malefics owning Kendras become auspicious. Rahu/Ketu effects.

### Chapter 20: Dasha-bhava-lord Bhukti (Dasas of Bhava-Lords and their Bhuktis)
Detailed effects of each planet's Dasha and Bhuktis. **Yogakaraka principle** (sl.45-53): lords of Kendra and Trikona in mutual relationship become Yogakarakas. Rahu/Ketu in Kendra/Trikona with lord-connection become Yogakarakas. Rajayoga timing in Yogakaraka Dasha. Maraka determination. Dasa varieties (Avarohini, Madhya, Arohini, Adhama). Mixed effects. Transit-based Dasa assessment.

### Chapter 21: Dasha-antara (Sub-divisions of Dasas)
Bhukti calculation for each planet's Dasha. Specific Bhukti effects within each Mahadasa.

### Chapter 22: Kalachakra Dasha
Kalachakra Dasha system. Nakshatra triads, Apasavya/Savya, planetary year assignments, Nakshatrapada formulas, initial Dasa calculation.

### Chapter 23: Ashtakavarga
Full Ashtakavarga system: benefic positions for each planet (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) counted from each of the 7 planets and Lagna. Effects of 0-8 bindus. Bhava-wise dot computation. Transit-timing via 8-division of Rasi. Sarvashtakavarga (>28 bindus = auspicious). Trikona Sodhana and Ekadhipatya Sodhana principles. Malefic in own sign promotes bhava; benefic owning dusthana harms bhava.

### Chapter 24: Horasara-okta Ashtakavarga
Ashtakavarga effects as stated in Hora Sara — father, mother, brothers, etc. from specific house counts.

### Chapter 25: Upagraha (Sub-planets)
Mandi and other Upagrahas (Gulika, etc.), their calculation and effects.

### Chapter 26: Gochara (Transits of Planets)
Comprehensive transit rules. Transits judged from the Moon's sign. Benefic transit houses for each planet. **Vedha rules** — for each benefic transit house, a corresponding Vedha house that nullifies the effect if occupied by another planet. Special exceptions (Sun-Saturn father-son Vedha exception). Detailed transit effects of Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn through all 12 houses from Janmarasi. Sarvato-bhadrachakra. Ashtakavarga-based transit assessment. Sade Sati and Saturn transit effects.

### Chapter 27: Pravrajya Yoga (Ascetic Yogas)
Conditions for taking ascetic orders: 10th lord with 4+ planets in Kendra/Trikona; Moon in Saturn's decanate aspected by Mars and Saturn; Moon in Mars Navamsa aspected by Saturn; Janmarasi lord aspected by Saturn. Classes of ascetics by planet (Sun = contemplative saint, Moon = pilgrim, Mars = Buddhist, Mercury = mendicant, Jupiter = Vedantin, Venus = hypocrite, Saturn = outcast). Rajayoga overriding ascetic yoga.

### Chapter 28: Upasamhara (Conclusion)
Summary of the 28 chapters. Author's colophon: Mantreswara, born in Sri Shalivari (Tirunelveli), worshipped Sukuntalamba Devi. The work is offered for the enjoyment of astrologers.

## Key Principles (unique to or emphasized in this translation)

1. **Neecha Bhanga explicitly enumerated as five types (Ch.7, sl.26-30):** The VB edition provides a numbered summary stating "There are five kinds of नीचभङ्गराजयोग" — (1) depression-sign lord or exaltation-sign lord in Kendra from Lagna/Moon; (2) both lords mutually in Kendra; (3) debilitated planet aspected by its sign lord; (4) lord of depression/exaltation sign in Kendra after the planet leaves debilitation; (5) the debilitated planet itself in Kendra from Lagna/Moon.
2. **Yogakaraka as a relational principle (Ch.20, sl.45-53):** Rather than a fixed ascendant table, the VB edition emphasizes that ANY lord of a Kendra in relationship with a lord of a Trikona becomes a Yogakaraka. Rahu/Ketu in Kendra/Trikona with lord-connection also become Yogakarakas.
3. **Kendradhipati Dosha principle unnamed but present (Ch.20, sl.50):** "Jupiter and Venus if they should happen to be owners of Kendras become powerful to cause evil" — the principle is stated but the term "Kendradhipati Dosha" is not used.
4. **Vedha system in transits (Ch.26):** The most detailed treatment — each benefic transit house has a corresponding Vedha house, with specific exceptions (e.g., Sun-Saturn father-son exception, Mercury-Moon exception).
5. **Vaiseshikamsas (Ch.3):** Seven grades of planetary excellence (Parijata through Airavata) from 2-9 Varga combinations, with a worked Jupiter example.
6. **Chandrakriya/Chandravasttha/Chandravela (Ch.4):** 60 + 12 + 36 = 108 specific effects derived from the Moon's nakshatra position, uniquely tabulated.
7. **Sarvato-bhadrachakra:** Explained fully here (mentioned only by name in other texts per the translator's preface).

## Named Yogas

### Ruchaka Yoga
- **Formation:** Mars in own sign (Mesha/Vrischika) or exaltation (Makara) in a Kendra
- **Effects:** Long face, wealthy through daring deeds, brave, victorious, arrogant, army leader, renowned
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.1-2
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic (Mahapurusha)
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical formation and effects in both editions.

### Bhadra Yoga
- **Formation:** Mercury in own sign or exaltation in a Kendra
- **Effects:** Long-lived, keen intellect, clean, praised by learned, leader, rich, clever in assembly
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.1-2
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Hamsa Yoga
- **Formation:** Jupiter in own sign or exaltation in a Kendra
- **Effects:** King extolled by the good, Sankha/Padma/Matsya/Ankusa rekhas on limbs, beautiful body, eats sumptuously, righteous
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.1, 3
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Malavya Yoga
- **Formation:** Venus in own sign or exaltation in a Kendra
- **Effects:** Strong limbs, resolute, wealthy, wife/children/good fortune, prosperous, good vehicles, famous, learned, unperturbed senses
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.1, 3
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Shasha Yoga
- **Formation:** Saturn in own sign or exaltation in a Kendra
- **Effects:** Extolled by all, good servants, strong, village headman or king, wicked disposition, intrigues with other women, usurps others' wealth
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.1, 4
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition. VB adds that these five yogas can be reckoned from the Moon as well as Lagna.

### Sunapha Yoga
- **Formation:** Planets other than Sun in the 2nd from the Moon
- **Effects:** King or equal, self-acquired property, renowned for wisdom and wealth
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.5-6
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Anapha Yoga
- **Formation:** Planets other than Sun in the 12th from the Moon
- **Effects:** Powerful, healthy, amiable, famous, material comforts, well-dressed, contented, happy
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.5-6
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Durudhara Yoga
- **Formation:** Planets other than Sun in both 2nd and 12th from the Moon
- **Effects:** Joys of life, wealth, vehicles, bountiful, faithful attendants
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.5, 7
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Kemadruma Yoga
- **Formation:** Absence of Sunapha, Anapha, and Durudhara (no planets flanking the Moon). Some say cancelled if Moon is in Kendra or associated with a planet, or if planets occupy Kendra from Moon.
- **Effects:** Obscure, miserable, base ways, penurious, menial, wicked — even if of princely race
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.5, 7
- **Cross-translation notes:** VB edition notes the cancellation conditions ("some declare there is no Kemadruma where a Kendra [is occupied] or the Moon is associated with a planet") — same as Kapoor.

### Kesari Yoga
- **Formation:** Moon in a Kendra position to Jupiter
- **Effects:** Destroys enemies like a lion, speaks loftily, passionate, long-lived, renowned, intelligent, conquers by valour
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.14, 16
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Mahabhagya Yoga
- **Formation:** Day birth (male) with Sun, Moon, Lagna in odd signs; OR night birth (female) with Sun, Moon, Lagna in even signs
- **Effects:** Causes immense pleasure, liberal, famous, ruler, 80-year life, spotless character; female: wealth, long-lived husband, sons, grandsons, lucky, well-behaved
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.14-15
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Sakata Yoga
- **Formation:** Moon in 6th, 8th, or 12th from Jupiter. Cancelled if Moon in Kendra from Lagna.
- **Effects:** Frequently loses fortune, may regain, ordinary, insignificant, mental grief, unhappy
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.14, 17
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition. VB explicitly states the cancellation condition.

### Vasumati Yoga
- **Formation:** All benefics in Upachaya houses (3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th) from Lagna or Moon
- **Effects:** Always at home, commands plenty of money
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.19-20
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Amala Yoga
- **Formation:** Benefics in the 10th from Lagna or Moon
- **Effects:** Rules the earth, wealthy, sons, famous, prosperous, prudent
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.19-20
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition. (Note: VB also describes a separate Amala yoga at sl.12 formed by benefics unaspected by malefics in 2nd from Lagna.)

### Lakshmi Yoga
- **Formation:** Lord of 9th and Venus in own/exaltation in Trikona or Kendra
- **Effects:** Sporting with amiable damsel, disease-free, wealthy, brilliant, protects people, blessings of Lakshmi, palanquins/horseback/elephant, best of kings, liberal
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.21, 24
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Saraswati Yoga
- **Formation:** Venus, Jupiter, Mercury in Kendra, Trikona, or 2nd house; Jupiter strong in exaltation/own/friendly house
- **Effects:** Highly intelligent, clever in drama/prose/versifying/accounts/poetics, skilled in sacred texts, fame over three worlds, wealthy, wife and children, fortunate, respected by kings
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.26-27
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Adhiyoga
- **Formation:** Benefics in 7th, 6th, and 8th from the Moon or Lagna
- **Effects:** Commander, minister, or ruler respectively; famous, prosperous, wealthy, long-lived, high-souled; lord of men, everlasting wealth, feeds relations, overthrows enemies, long life, renowned
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.42-43
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Viparita Raja Yoga (Harsha, Sarala, Vimala)
- **Formation:** Lords of dusthanas (6th, 8th, 12th) occupying dusthanas. Specifically: 6th lord in 6th = Harsha; 8th lord in 8th = Sarala; 12th lord in 12th = Vimala. (Part of the 12 yogas from bhava lords in dusthanas, sl.57)
- **Effects:** Harsha — happiness, enjoyment, good fortune, strong constitution, overcomes enemies, friend of illustrious people. Sarala — long-lived, resolute, fearless, prosperous, learning/children/riches, success, overcomes foes, pure, celebrated. Vimala — spends little, saves much, good to all, happy, independent, respectable profession, renowned.
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.57, 63, 65, 69
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic (arising from malefic placement)
- **Cross-translation notes:** The VB edition does not use the term "Viparita Raja Yoga" explicitly but describes the identical principle. Harsha, Sarala, Vimala are part of a larger set of 12 yogas (one per bhava lord in dusthana). The Kapoor edition groups these three as "Viparita Raja Yoga" — same underlying concept.

### Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga (5 Types)
- **Formation:** A debilitated planet whose debilitation is cancelled, producing Rajayoga/kingship. Five types enumerated:
  1. Lord of the depression sign or the planet exalted in that sign is in Kendra from Lagna or Moon (sl.26)
  2. Lords of the depression sign and the exaltation sign are mutually in Kendra positions (sl.27)
  3. The debilitated planet is aspected by the lord of that (depression) sign (sl.28)
  4. Lord of the depression sign or exaltation-sign lord in Kendra from Lagna/Moon (after the planet leaves debilitation) (sl.29)
  5. The debilitated planet itself is in Kendra with respect to Lagna or Moon (sl.30)
- **Effects:** King, just ruler, emperor, respected by other kings, virtuous, mighty, famous, affluent
- **Source:** Ch.7, sl.26-30
- **Cross-translation notes:** **KEY DIFFERENCE:** The VB edition explicitly enumerates FIVE types. The Kapoor edition graph contains only 4 type nodes (type1-type4), though its hyperedge label mentions "Five Types." The VB edition's type 5 (debilitated planet itself in Kendra from Lagna/Moon) corresponds to Kapoor's type4. The VB type 4 (lord in Kendra after planet leaves debilitation, sl.29) appears to be an additional nuance not separately captured in the Kapoor graph. The VB edition provides translator's notes on alternative readings of "तदुच्चनाथ" (sl.26).

### Raja Yoga (Basic)
- **Formation:** Lords of 9th and 10th in conjunction in an auspicious Bhava
- **Effects:** Kingship with elephants, horses, palanquins, conch shell, umbrella, praised by bards
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.37-38
- **Cross-translation notes:** Identical to Kapoor edition.

### Sankha Yoga
- **Formation:** Lords of a Kendra and a Trikona in conjunction in an auspicious Bhava
- **Effects:** Enjoys all comforts with beautiful damsels
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.37-38
- **Cross-translation notes:** Not separately captured in Kapoor graph; expands the Raja Yoga concept.

### Parivartana Yogas (66 total)
- **Formation:** Two planets mutually exchanging houses (each in the other's sign). 30 Dainya (involving 6th/8th/12th lords), 8 Khala (involving 3rd lord), 28 Maha (remaining).
- **Effects:** Dainya — fool, reviles others, sinful, tormented by enemies. Khala — alternately goes astray and gentle, fluctuating fortune. Maha — repository of Sri's blessings, wealthy, gold ornaments, royal presents, vehicles, children.
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.32-34
- **Cross-translation notes:** VB edition provides the full enumeration of all 66 combinations — more detailed than the Kapoor graph.

### Sankhya Yogas (7 types)
- **Formation:** Seven planets occupying 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1 signs respectively → Vallaki (Veena), Dama, Pasa, Kedara, Sula, Yuga, Gola
- **Effects:** Veena — fond of dance/music, wealthy; Dama — liberal, king, benefactor; Pasa — opulent, enjoyment; Kedara — wealth, agricultural lands; Sula — cruel, angry, indigent; Yuga — heretical, without wealth; Gola — without wealth, sinful, low associates, bad artisan, short-lived
- **Source:** Ch.6, sl.39-41
- **Cross-translation notes:** Not in Kapoor graph; unique detail from VB edition.

## Dasha Systems
- **Vimshottari Dasha:** Primary system (Ch.19-21). Dasha years: Sun 6, Moon 10, Mars 7, Rahu 18, Jupiter 16, Saturn 19, Mercury 17, Ketu 7, Venus 20. Bhukti (Antardasa) calculation detailed. Dasa varieties: Avarohini (descending from exaltation), Madhya (middling), Arohini (rising from depression), Adhama (worst, in depression/inimical). Mixed effects (Misra phala) when planet occupies auspicious sign/Amsa despite being eclipsed/debilitated.
- **Kalachakra Dasha:** Ch.22. Nakshatra triads, Apasavya/Savya cycles, planetary year assignments, Nakshatrapada formulas.
- **Yogakaraka Dasa timing:** Rajayoga begins in Yogakaraka Dasa during Karaka Bhuktis (Ch.20, sl.54).

## Transit (Gochar) Rules
- **Base point:** All transits judged from the Moon's sign (Janmarasi) — Ch.26, sl.1.
- **Benefic transit houses (from Moon):** Sun in 6th, 3rd, 10th; Moon in 3rd, 10th, 6th, 7th, 1st; Jupiter in 7th, 9th, 2nd, 5th; Mars/Saturn in 6th, 3rd; Mercury in 6th, 2nd, 4th, 10th, 8th; all planets in 11th; Venus everywhere except 10th, 7th, 6th.
- **Vedha system:** For each benefic transit house, a corresponding Vedha house nullifies the effect if occupied by another planet. Key exceptions: Sun-Saturn (father-son, no Vedha); Moon-Mercury exception; Mars-Sun exception for Saturn transits.
- **Detailed 12-house effects:** Given for Sun (sl.9-11), Moon (sl.12), Mars (sl.13-16), Mercury (sl.17), Jupiter (sl.18-20), Venus, Saturn through all 12 houses from Janmarasi.
- **Sarvato-bhadrachakra:** Explained fully (uniquely detailed in this text per translator's preface).
- **Ashtakavarga-integrated transits:** Ch.23, sl.13-16 — transit timing via 8-division of Rasi; effects based on bindu count.

## Ashtakavarga
- **Definition (Ch.23, sl.1):** Combination of benefic/malefic dots in a planet's chart with reference to 8 sources (7 planets + Lagna).
- **Planet-by-planet benefic positions (sl.3-9):** Full tables for Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn — counting from each of the 7 planets and Lagna.
- **Bindu effects (sl.11):** 0 bindus = loss of life; 1 = destruction; 2 = expenditure; 3 = fear; 4 = fear; 5 = accomplishment; 6 = acquisition of damsel; 7 = gain of wealth; 8 = gaining kingdom.
- **Bhava-wise computation (sl.12):** Each planet's sign as Lagna; dots in each bhava predict effects.
- **Transit timing (sl.16-19):** Rasi divided into 8 equal divisions; lords are (south to north): Lagna, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Sun, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn. Effects fructify during transit over the division owned by the dot-yielding planet.
- **Sarvashtakavarga (sl.20):** Sum of all 7 planetary Ashtakavargas. Rasi with >28 bindus = auspicious for transits; below 28 = danger/sorrow.
- **Trikona Sodhana & Ekadhipatya Sodhana:** Implied in the rectification process (sl.21-22, with cross-reference to Jatakaparijata pp.633-663).
- **Special rules (sl.23-24):** Malefic in own sign promotes bhava; benefic owning dusthana harms bhava even if exalted.

## Cross-Text Relations (vs BPHS, Hora Sara, GPD)
- **Agrees with BPHS on:** Planetary significations, exaltation/debilitation signs, Shadbala framework, Vimshottari Dasha years, Ashtakavarga benefic positions (cross-referenced to Parasara in footnotes), Pancha Mahapurusha formations.
- **Differs from BPHS on:** VB notes that for Jupiter's Ashtakavarga, "According to Varahamihira, 1st, 4th, 7th, 8th, 10th, 11th and 12th places from Jupiter" differ from the standard listing. VB also notes Parasara's variation for Venus from Mars.
- **Agrees with Hora Sara on:** Ch.24 explicitly presents "the Ashtakavarga effects as stated in Horasara" — confirming textual continuity.
- **Unique to this text:** Chandrakriya/Chandravasttha/Chandravela system; Sarvato-bhadrachakra; the 108 Moon-based effects; the explicit five-type Neecha Bhanga enumeration; the complete Vedha exception tables.

## Conflicts & Ambiguities
- **Neecha Bhanga type count:** VB explicitly says "five kinds" but types 1 and 4 (sl.26 and sl.29) appear similar (both involve the depression/exaltation sign lord in Kendra). The distinction is whether the planet remains in debilitation (type 1) or has left it (type 4). The OCR garbling of sl.29-30 makes the precise distinction difficult to parse.
- **Kemadruma cancellation:** VB notes "some declare" cancellation by Kendra occupation or association — indicating a disputed point among authorities.
- **Ashtakavarga benefic positions:** VB footnotes note variations from Varahamihira (for Jupiter) and Parasara (for Venus from Mars), indicating textual variant traditions.
- **Yogakaraka for specific ascendants:** The VB edition gives the general principle (Kendra + Trikona lord relationship) rather than a fixed table; specific ascendant assignments (Saturn for Taurus/Libra, Mars for Cancer/Leo, Venus for Capricorn/Aquarius) must be derived from the principle. The Kapoor graph presents these as fixed nodes.

## Extraction Notes
- **OCR quality:** The source is OCR'd from a 1937 printed edition. Quality is moderate — Sanskrit Devanagari is frequently garbled (e.g., "ADHYAYA ४" for "ADHYAYA V", "ADHYAYA VIIL" for "ADHYAYA VIII", "ADHYAYA XVIL" for "ADHYAYA XVII"). English text is generally legible but with character recognition errors (e.g., "970४0" for "Sloka", "फ" vs "फल"). Numerals are sometimes in Devanagari.
- **Page structure:** Text is segmented by "## Page N" headers (362+ pages). Chapter boundaries are clearly marked by "ADHYAYA [roman numeral]" headers and "Thus ends the Nth Adhyaya on '[title]'" colophons.
- **Chapter numbering gaps:** OCR missed the headers for Adhyayas V (rendered as "ADHYAYA ४" with Devanagari 4), VIII (as "VIIL"), and XVII (as "XVIL"). These were identified by their colophons and content.
- **Gaps:** Pages 2-6 are blank (front matter). The Slokanukramanika (verse index, pp.325-340+) is extensive but heavily garbled in OCR. The English/Sanskrit indices at the end are partially legible.
- **Cross-reference markers:** The translator frequently uses "vid: XV-30 supra" style references to Chapter XV Sloka 30 for the planetary relationship definition (mutual aspect, exchange, conjunction) — this is the key "Sambandha" (relationship) definition referenced in the Yogakaraka discussion.
