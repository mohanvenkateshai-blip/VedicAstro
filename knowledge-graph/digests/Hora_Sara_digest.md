# Hora Sara — Structured Digest

## Bibliographic Metadata
- **Title:** Hora Sara (Horasara / होरासारः)
- **Author:** Prithuyasas (son of Varaha Mihira; end-of-chapter colophons read "श्रीवराहमिहिरसुतश्रीपृथुयशोविरचिते")
- **Translator/Commentator:** R. Santhanam (also translator of Saravali, Brihat Parasara Hora Sastra, Nashta Jatakam, Garga Hora)
- **Edition/Publisher:** Ranjan Publications, 16 Ansari Road, Daryaganj, New Delhi-110002 (1982)
- **Approximate date of composition:** ca. 550–600 CE (Prithuyasas is dated ~1400 years before the 1982 edition per translator's note, placing him in the late Gupta / post-Varahamihira period)
- **Source file:** extracted_markdown/Hora_Sara.md
- **Total chapters/sections:** 32 Adhyayas, 1209 slokas (per translator's note: 12 Rasis × 9 planets symbolism)
- **Scope:** A foundational classical horoscopy text that distills and expands Brihat Jataka. Covers: rasi divisions, planetary natures and dignities, planetary strength, conception/birth, balarishta (infant mortality) and longevity yogas, multiple dasha systems (Pindayurdaya, Amsayurdaya, Naisargika, Ashtakavarga, Vimshottari, Moola), dasha and antardasa phala, Ashtakavarga (with full bindu allocations), Chandra/Nabhasa yogas, Raja yogas, bhava effects, profession, planetary conjunctions, female horoscopy, death (Niryaniika), lost horoscopy (Nashta Jataka), nakshatra qualities, Moon-sign and Navamsa effects, and horoscopic canons. Notable for its extensive Ashtakavarga chapter and its detailed treatment of death-timing via transits.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

### Chapter 1: Rasi Vibhaga (Divisions of Rasis)
The 12 rasis from Aries to Pisces are listed with their forms, habitats (watery/land/village/forest/ battlefield), quadruped/biped/reptile classifications, day/night and movable/fixed/dual signs, caste (Brahmin/Kshatriya/Vaishya/Shudra), body parts of Kalapurusha, colors, and short/medium/long ascension. Defines Gandantha junctions (Revati–Ashwini, Ashlesha–Magha, Jyeshtha–Mula), Navamsa/Dwadasamsa/Drekkana/Trimsamsa divisions, the 12 Bhava synonyms (Tanu, Artha, Sahaja…), Kendra/Panaphara/Apoklima/Upachaya/Chaturasra/Shatkona, and alternative names for Lagna and the 8th/9th/10th/12th houses.

### Chapter 2: Grahalakshanas (Planetary Characteristics)
Lists rasi and Navamsa lords, Hora lords (Sun for odd-sign first half, Moon for even), directional rulers, exaltation signs and deep-exaltation degrees (Sun Aries 10°, Moon Taurus 3°, Mars Capricorn 28°, Mercury Virgo 15°, Jupiter Cancer 5°, Venus Pisces 27°, Saturn Libra 20°) with debilitation 7th therefrom. Moolatrikona and Swakshetra zones given per planet. Defines natural friendships (lords of 3,6,7,10,11 from Moolatrikona are enemies), temporary friendships, planetary aspects (Mars special 4/8, Jupiter 5/9, Saturn 3/10), genders, castes, gunas (Sattvic/Rajasic/Tamasic), humours (bile/wind/phlegm), colors, body descriptions, ages, and synonyms for all 9 grahas including Rahu/Ketu.

### Chapter 3: Bala Nirupana (Evaluation of Planetary Strengths)
Sign-by-sign strength conditions for each planet (own/exaltation/friendly sign, day/night, direction, portion of sign). Gives Digbala (Mercury & Jupiter in Lagna, Moon & Venus in 4th, Sun & Mars in 10th, Saturn in 7th), relative strength ratios (Saturn<Mars<Mercury<Jupiter<Venus<Moon<Sun<Rahu), and the principle that combust planets fail to deliver bhava/yoga/dasha results. Notes the tripod of strength: Lagna + Sun + Moon.

### Chapter 4: Nisheka Janma (Conception and Birth)
Conditions for conception (Moon in Anupachaya aspected by Mars for menstruation; Moon in Upachaya aspected by male benefic Jupiter for conception); 16-night fertile window after menses. Yogas for male/female/twin/triplet births based on odd/even signs and Navamsas. Month-by-month fetal development with planetary rulers (Venus→Mars→Jupiter→Sun→Moon→Saturn→Mercury→Lagna lord→Moon→Sun). Rules for delivery timing from Prasna Lagna Dwadasamsa and Navamsa.

### Chapter 5: Arishtas (Evils at Birth)
Sun = father, Moon = mother (with day/night birth variants per Brihat Jataka). Combinations for father/mother death, absence of father at birth, illicit birth (Jaraja yoga — cancellation if Jupiter aspects Moon/Lagna). Moles and scars on body limbs (decanate mapping). Three kinds of infant death (parental, Balagraha, planetary). Gandanta nakshatra birth destroys family but survivor becomes king (examples: Ramakrishna Dalmia, Mahatma Gandhi). Fateful degrees for Lagna and Moon. Cancellation of Arishta by strong Lagna lord in Kendra/Trikona or Jupiter's aspect.

### Chapter 6: Arishta Yoga (Longevity — Special Rules)
Enumerates yogas fixing life-span: 20, 25, 30, 32, 36, 40, 48, 50, 52, 58, 60, 65, 70, 80, 85, 100 years, and extraordinary spans (yuga, 2000 years, "uncountable"). Defines short/medium/long life by planet distribution across the four quadrants. Notes that for short-life and extraordinary-life nativities, dasha-based longevity cannot be computed.

### Chapter 7: Ayurdaya (Longevity Computation)
Four dasha systems for longevity: (1) Pindayurdaya — deep-exaltation planets give 19/25/15/12/15/21/20 years (Sun→Saturn), half at deep debilitation, proportionate between; (2) Amsayurdaya — Navamsa-based, used when Lagna is strongest; (3) Naisargikayu — used when Moon is strongest; (4) Ashtakavarga dasa — declared "best of all." Deductions for combustion, enemy signs, planetary war. Trinal reduction (Trikona Sodhana), Ekadhipatya Sodhana, and Mandala Sodhana (cap 12 dots per sign) detailed.

### Chapter 8: Dasa Phala Nirupana (Dasa Effects Framework)
Order of dashas: strongest of Lagna/Sun/Moon first, then Kendra planets, then non-Kendra. Defines Arohini (planet approaching exaltation — full effects) and Avarohini (departing exaltation — diminishing effects). Naisargika dasa order: Moon→Mars→Mercury→Venus→Jupiter→Sun→Saturn. Extensive note on Vimshottari conversion from Savana mana to Sauramana (120-year cycle, 365d 15gh 31li 5vi per solar year), with full tables of Mahadasa-bhukti durations in Sauramana and a Kali-yuga Savana calendar.

### Chapter 9: Sun's Dasa Effects
General Sun dasa: foreign residence, forests/forts, wealth via Brahmins/gods/fire/kings/sastras/medicines; proficiencies, eye/abdomen/tooth/ear diseases. Sign-by-sign Sun dasa effects (Aries through Pisces) plus exaltation, debilitation, combust, and 8th-house placements.

### Chapter 10: Moon's Dasa Effects
Six categories of Moon dasa (3 Arohini, 3 Avarohini) based on waxing/waning, ocean waves, and departure from exaltation/debilitation. Sign-by-sign effects, including Vargothamamsa effects (Pisces and Aquarius noted).

### Chapter 11: Mars' Dasa Effects
Mars always yields medium exaltation-like effects regardless of orientation. Sign-by-sign effects including deep-exaltation (Capricorn 28°), debilitation, combust, Vargothama, and 8th-house placements.

### Chapter 12: Mercury's Dasa Effects
General Mercury dasa: teaching, relatives, agriculture, yagna, sculpture, music. Sign-by-sign effects including exaltation (Virgo), debilitation, combust, Vargothama, and 10th/11th/4th house placements.

### Chapter 13: Jupiter's Dasa Effects
General Jupiter dasa: Vedas, four Purusharthas, learning, worship, medicines, yagna, conveyances, lands, wife, sons, royal status. Sign-by-sign effects including exaltation (Cancer), Moolatrikona (Sagittarius), debilitation (Capricorn), combust, and 8th-house placements.

### Chapter 14: Venus' Dasa Effects (per contents)
Sign-by-sign Venus dasa effects covering exaltation, debilitation, Vargothama, combust, and 8th-house placements.

### Chapter 15: Saturn's Dasa Effects
Sign-by-sign Saturn dasa effects including exaltation (Libra), own signs (Capricorn, Aquarius), debilitation (Aries), combust, Vargothama, and 8th-house placements.

### Chapter 16: Miscellaneous Dasa Effects
Combined effects, Rahu/Ketu dasa effects, and miscellaneous rules for planets in various Bhavas during their dashas.

### Chapter 17: Ashtakavarga Effects
Full bindu allocations for all 7 planets' Bhinnashtakavarga (Sun=48, Moon=49, Mars=39, Mercury=54, Jupiter=56, Venus=52, Saturn=39; total 337). Rules for interpreting dasa effects based on bindu count (0 = disease/loss; 1-3 = loss; 4 = medium; 5+ = all desires fulfilled). Extensive Bhava significations (12 houses with detailed karakatwas). Applications: father's death via Saturn transit to 9th from Sun (with Ashtakavarga calculation), mother's death, marriage-month avoidance (zero-bindu signs in Sun's AV), profession direction, progeny count from Jupiter's AV in 5th house.

### Chapter 18: Antardasa Phala (Sub-Period Effects)
Moola dasa scheme: planet conjunct dasa lord gets half the period; planets in 5th/9th get one-third; 7th gets one-seventh; 4th/8th get one-fourth. Antardasa results for all 9 mahadasha-antardasha combinations (Sun-Sun through Saturn-Venus). Rules for death-timing: 6th/8th lord mutual periods, Rahu in Lagna's Navamsa lord's dasa, 22nd decanate transit by Jupiter, Sun's transit to computed points (Chanakya's method using Sun×Rahu longitudes).

### Chapter 19: Chandra and Nabhasa Yogas
Lunar yogas: Sunapha (2nd from Moon), Anapha (12th), Durudhura (both), Adhiyoga (benefics in 6/7/8 from Moon/Lagna). Kemadruma (Moon without planets in 2/12 or Kendras). Solar yogas: Vesi (2nd from Sun), Vasi (12th), Ubhayachari (both). Lagna yogas: Sushubha, Ashubha, Karthari, Lagnadhi. Structural yogas: Parvatha, Dhwaja, Kundala. Nabhasa yogas by planet distribution: Gola (1 sign), Yuga (2), Shula (3), Kedara (4), Pasa (5), Dama (6), Veena (7). Kendra yogas: Mangala, Madhya, Kleeba. Rajju/Musala/Nala (movable/fixed/dual signs). Mala/Sarpa (benefics/malefics in Kendras). Shatpada, Varaha, Kantaka, Hala, Sringataka, Sakata, Vihaga. Sankha, Chakra, Samudra, Mahendra.

### Chapter 20: Raja Yogas
~28 Raja Yoga formulations: Moon/Lagna in Vargothama aspected by 4+ planets; Venus in Ashwini Lagna aspected by all; 3+ planets in own/exaltation (avoiding debilitation Navamsa) with one in Lagna; Moon+Mars in Lagna/9th/10th with strong Sun+Jupiter; Jupiter aspecting Mercury; Moon in friendly Navamsa aspected by Venus (night birth) or Jupiter (day birth); Leo Lagna with Mercury in Virgo; Pushkala Yoga (Moon-sign lord + Lagna lord in friendly Kendra aspecting Lagna); Lagna lord exalted in Kendra alone; Mars in Lagna of Aries/Leo/Sagittarius aspected by friend; Mars in 10th in Ashwini/Anuradha/Dhanishta; Bhagya Yoga (odd signs for day-born males, even for night-born females — similar to Phaladeepika's Maha Bhagya).

### Chapter 21: Planetary Effects for Bhavas
Bhava-by-bhava effects for each planet (Sun in 12 houses, Moon in 12, etc.) — covering all 7 planets plus Rahu/Ketu across all 12 Bhavas from Lagna.

### Chapter 22: Karma Jiva (Livelihood)
Profession deduced from 10th house from Lagna/Sun/Moon and the Navamsa lord of the 10th lord. Source-by-source: Sun = fruits/trees/metals/sculpture/betting; Moon = pearls/ships/agriculture/clothes/cows; Mars = weapons/fire/medicine/Kshatriyas; Mercury = writing/math/dance/education; Jupiter = learning/priesthood/mantras/astrology/royal favor; Venus = cows/horses/women/medicines/singing; Saturn = fruits/roots/labor/servants/grain/sesamum/salt. Movable/fixed/dual Navamsa of 10th lord determines home vs. foreign earning.

### Chapter 23: Conjunctions of Two or More Planets
Pair-wise conjunction effects (Sun-Mercury, Sun-Mars, Sun-Jupiter, Sun-Venus, Sun-Saturn, Moon-Mars, Moon-Mercury, Moon-Jupiter, Moon-Venus, Moon-Saturn, Mars-Mercury, Mars-Jupiter, Mars-Venus, Mars-Saturn, Mercury-Jupiter, Mercury-Venus, Mercury-Saturn, Jupiter-Venus, Jupiter-Saturn, Venus-Saturn) with house-specific variations, plus triple conjunctions (Sun-Moon-Mars through Jupiter-Venus-Saturn).

### Chapter 24: Jataka Bhanga (Adverse Combinations)
Avayogas that nullify Raja Yogas: Jala yoga, alternate Kemadruma (9th lord in 12th, 12th lord in 2nd weak, malefics in 3rd), Kulapamsana yoga, Bhikshashana yoga, Chandala yoga, Unmada/Paisacha/Mahagada yogas (insanity). The single Sun in deep fall (Libra 10°) cancels 1000 Raja Yogas. Venus in Virgo Navamsa destroys empires. Exchange of Sun-Moon Navamsas ruins all Raja Yogas. Sanyasa/ascetic yogas detailed.

### Chapter 25: Stree Jataka (Female Horoscopy)
Same yogas apply to both sexes; female-specific results restricted to widowhood (8th house), husband's luck (7th), appearance (Lagna), children/wealth (9th). Detailed Trimsamsa effects for each of the 12 Lagna signs × 5 Trimsamsa lords (Mars/Mercury/Jupiter/Venus/Saturn). Combinations for barrenness, diseased vagina, second marriage, unchastity, widowhood.

### Chapter 26: Nairyaniika (End of Life / Death)
Cause of death from 8th house occupant (Sun=fire, Moon=water, Mars=weapon, Mercury=fall, Jupiter=fever, Venus=indigestion, Saturn=hunger). Sign-specific death causes (Aries=fever/poison/bile; Taurus=three humours/weapons; Gemini=cough/breathing; etc.). Place of death (movable=foreign, fixed=own house, dual=on move). Death alongside many (mass casualties), with son, with wife. After-death destiny: Jupiter's sign/Mercury's decanate = heaven; Venus/Moon = Pitruloka; Sun/Mars = mortal world; Ketu = Mrityuloka; Mercury = animal birth; Saturn/Rahu = demon rebirth. Jupiter in Lagna end or Sun in Jupiter's sign Lagna = final emancipation.

### Chapter 27: Nashta Jataka (Lost Horoscopy)
Method to reconstruct unknown birth data from Prasna Lagna. Determines: Uttarayana/Dakshinayana from Lagna half; Ritu from Lagna lord or decanate lord; month from decanate portion; day/night birth from specific signs; ghatis from Sun-rise from degrees traversed; natal star from Prasna-Udaya Lagna difference × 2 + query star, divided by 27; Janma Rasi from ray (kirana) calculations.

### Chapter 28: Nakshatra Shila (Qualities of Constellations)
Personality/physical/fortune effects for each of the 27 nakshatras from Ashwini to Revati. Notes that birth-star effects are amplified when aspected/conjoined by benefics. Lists asterisms to be avoided for females (Ardra, Ashlesha, Shatabhisha, Pushya, Mula, Chitra, Jyeshtha, Krittika) due to grief to brothers/sons. Same-nakshatra children: son of father's star causes mother's death and vice versa; girls exempt.

### Chapter 29: Rashi Shila (Moon Sign Effects)
Personality, physique, fortune, and life-course for Moon in each of the 12 signs from Aries to Pisces. Effects modified by planetary conjunction/aspect.

### Chapter 30: Amsha Shila (Navamsa Effects)
Effects of Lagna falling in each of the 12 Navamsas (Aries navamsa through Pisces navamsa), with critical/dangerous years listed for each (e.g., Aries navamsa: 12th/25th/50th/65th year; Taurus: 22nd/10th/23rd/72nd; etc.). General critical years: 59th, 32nd, 8th. Vargothama planets give doubled results.

### Chapter 31: Nakshatra Jataka (Vimshottari Dasa)
Vimshottari dasa years: Sun 6, Moon 10, Mars 7, Rahu 18, Jupiter 16, Saturn 19, Mercury 17, Ketu 7, Venus 20 (total 120), counted from Krittika. Dasa order from birth star (or Lagna star if stronger). End-periods of Sun/Mars/Saturn/Rahu dashas bring disease, wealth-loss, disputes. Ketu dasa effects resemble Mars; Rahu resembles Saturn. Author declares Vimshottari "acceptable to all" and most reliable (नक्षत्रजातकं प्रोक्तं सर्वेषां सम्मतं नृणाम्).

### Chapter 32: Jataka Lakshanas (Horoscopic Canons)
Procedural canons for the astrologer: auspicious setting, worship of Ganesa and planets, observation of omens (snake, chameleon, skull = evil; lizard cry on left, sneezing on non-left = evil; fox/buffalo/cow sounds = death). Calculate planetary positions via Pitamaha Siddhanta. Navamsa = longitude × 9 ÷ 12. Sun/Mars give beginning effects; Mercury/Jupiter/Saturn end effects; Moon rising effects; Venus motion-dependent. Concluding invocations.

## Key Principles (unique to or emphasized by this text)

1. **Tripod of Horoscope:** Lagna + Sun + Moon constitute the foundational strength; if the Sun is in deep fall, all other yogas become ineffective (Ch 24 sloka 26); if the Moon is in deep fall, all yogas become ineffective — even a single debilitated Sun can nullify 1000 Raja Yogas.
2. **Six categories of Moon dasa (Arohini/Avarohini):** Three Arohini (waxing tithi, rising ocean waves, departing debilitation) and three Avarohini (waning tithi, falling waves, departing exaltation) — unique to Horasara.
3. **Ashtakavarga as the best dasha system:** Ch 7 explicitly declares Ashtakavarga dasa "उत्तमोत्तमः" (best of the best) for longevity computation, with full bindu totals (337 total across 7 planets).
4. **Mandala Sodhana:** Cap of 12 bindus per sign in Sarvashtakavarga after Trikona and Ekadhipatya reductions — distinctive rule.
5. **Sauramana conversion for Vimshottari:** Detailed conversion tables from Savana mana (360-day) to Saura mana (365.25-day) years for precise dasha timing, with Kali-yuga day-count calendar.
6. **Pinda/Graha multipliers for Ashtakavarga:** Rasi multipliers (Aries 7, Taurus 10, Gemini 8, … Pisces 12) and Graha multipliers (Jupiter 10, Mars 8, Venus 7, Mercury 5, others 5) — specific to Horasara's longevity method.
7. **Marana Pada (Arudha of 8th house):** Distinctive treatment of the Arudha/Pada of the 8th house as "Marana Pada" for death-timing, calculated per Jaimini Sutras.
8. **Moola Dasha antardasa scheme:** Antardasa shares allocated by position from dasha lord — conjunct = 1/2, 5th/9th = 1/3, 7th = 1/7, 4th/8th = 1/4 (other houses get no antardasa) — used for all sub-period effects in Ch 18.
9. **Gandanta survival = kingship:** Birth in Gandanta nakshatra (Revati–Ashwini, Ashlesha–Magha, Jyeshtha–Mula junctions) destroys the family, but if the child survives, he becomes a king with elephants and horses (exemplified by Ramakrishna Dalmia and Mahatma Gandhi).
10. **Death-timing via Chanakya's method:** Multiply Sun's longitude (in minutes) by Rahu's longitude (in minutes), divide by 21600, add to natal Sun — the resultant sign/trine in Solar transit causes death.
11. **Trimsamsa for female horoscopy:** Detailed 12-sign × 5-Trimsamsa-lord matrix (Ch 25) giving female-specific results (chastity, barrenness, servant status, etc.) — most elaborate Trimsamsa treatment among classics.
12. **22nd decanate (Dwadasamsa of 8th from Lagna/Moon):** Jupiter's transit to the 22nd decanate from Lagna or Moon causes death.

**Rahu/Ketu** (notes from Ch 2 sloka 8): Multiple classical opinions — Aquarius/Scorpio own signs (Vriddha Samhita); Taurus/Scorpio exaltation (Bhavartha Ratnakara); Gemini/Sagittarius exaltation (Syama Sangraham). Horasara itself does not fix Rahu/Ketu dignities.

**Gunas:** Sun/Jupiter/Moon = Sattvic; Mercury/Venus = Rajasic; Mars/Saturn/Rahu = Tamasic.

**Humours:** Sun & Mars = bile; Moon & Saturn = wind; Venus & Moon = wind+phlegm; Mercury = mixed.

**Aspects (special):** Mars — full on 4th/8th; Jupiter — full on 5th/9th; Saturn — full on 3rd/10th. All planets aspect 7th fully, 4th/8th at 3/4, 5th/9th at 1/2, 3rd/10th at 1/4.

## Named Yogas

### Sunapha Yoga
- **Formation:** Planet (other than Sun) in 2nd from Moon
- **Effects:** Self-earned wealth, wisdom, dear to king, king himself, sastra-knower, commentator, virtuous, famous
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 1–2
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_sunapha) and Phaladeepika (phaladeepika_yoga_sunapha); identical formation and effects.

### Anapha Yoga
- **Formation:** Planet (other than Sun) in 12th from Moon
- **Effects:** Political wisdom, moral training, steadfast, knows many sastras, enormous wealth, attracts women, honored
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 1, 3
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_anapha) and Phaladeepika (phaladeepika_yoga_anapha).

### Durudhura Yoga
- **Formation:** Planets (other than Sun) in both 2nd and 12th from Moon
- **Effects:** Luxuries, wealth, eloquent, fond of battles, army chief, bewildered, king, charitable, cattle
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 1, 4
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_duradhura) and Phaladeepika (phaladeepika_yoga_durudhura). Note: Horasara spells "Durudhura" (दुरुधुरा) where BPHS uses "Duradhura".

### Adhiyoga
- **Formation:** Benefics in 6th, 7th, 8th from Moon (or Lagna), free from Sun's company and malefic aspect
- **Effects:** Minister, commander, king, wealth, courage, long life, destroys enemies
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 1, 5–6
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_adhi) and Phaladeepika (phaladeepika_yoga_adhiyoga). Horasara notes benefic participants give effortless results; malefics require effort.

### Kemadruma Yoga
- **Formation:** Moon without planets (except Sun) in 2nd, 12th, or both; additionally Moon's Kendras unoccupied and no planet in Kendra of Lagna
- **Effects:** Even a king becomes a beggar; poverty, misery, dependence on others
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 8; variant in Ch 24, sloka 3
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Malefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_kemadruma) and Phaladeepika (phaladeepika_yoga_kemadruma) on the core definition. Horasara adds a second Kemadruma variant (9th lord in 12th, 12th lord in 2nd weak, malefics in 3rd) in Ch 24 sloka 3.

### Vesi (Vesi) Yoga
- **Formation:** Planet (other than Moon) in 2nd from Sun
- **Effects:** Slow walk, soft-spoken, hopeless eyes, dear to men, balanced income/outflow
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 9–10
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Depends on participating planets
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_vesi). Note: Horasara uses "Vesi" (वेसि) spelling where BPHS/Phaladeepika use "Vosi" (वोसि) for 12th-from-Sun. Horasara's "Vasi" = 12th from Sun, "Vesi" = 2nd from Sun.

### Vasi Yoga
- **Formation:** Planet (other than Moon) in 12th from Sun
- **Effects:** If malefics: sinful, defective-limbed, sleepy, laborious; if benefics strong: all comforts and wealth
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 9, 11
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Depends
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_vosi); Horasara's "Vasi" = BPHS's "Vosi".

### Ubhayachari Yoga
- **Formation:** Planets (other than Moon) in both 2nd and 12th from Sun
- **Effects:** Talkative, wise, strong, leader, dear to king, enthusiastic, eloquent (if benefic-caused)
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 9, 12
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Depends
- **Cross-text notes:** Agrees with BPHS (bphs_yoga_ubhayachari).

### Lagnadhi Yoga
- **Formation:** Benefics in 6th, 7th, 8th from Lagna, free from malefic aspect; malefics not in 4th
- **Effects:** Minister, army chief, king, many wives, humble, long life, virtuous, enemy-free
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 17–18
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Benefic
- **Cross-text notes:** Supplements the Adhi Yoga concept (bphs_yoga_adhi) applied specifically to Lagna.

### Sushubha Yoga
- **Formation:** Planet (other than luminaries) in 2nd from Lagna
- **Effects:** Wealthy, attached to women, principled, ever-active, fickle, luxurious, unreasonable, head of monetary transactions
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 13–14
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Depends on planet

### Ashubha Yoga
- **Formation:** Planet (other than luminaries) in 12th from Lagna
- **Effects:** Strong body, caste leader, enthusiastic, speaker, helpful, charitable, dear to all, famous
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 13, 15
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Depends

### Karthari Yoga
- **Formation:** Malefics in both 2nd and 12th from Lagna (like scissors cutting)
- **Effects:** Angry, grieved, hates parents, lacks enthusiasm, lives abroad, danger from poison/fire/weapons
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 13, 16
- **Benefic/Malefic:** Malefic

### Parvatha Yoga
- **Formation:** Planets in 1st, 7th, 10th, 4th (Kendras); none in 12th and 8th; 9th occupied
- **Effects:** Rules earth, virtuous, modest, establishes villages/towns/cities, fame till Yuga-end
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 19–20
- **Cross-text notes:** CONTRADICTS Phaladeepika (Ch 6 sloka 35: Lagna lord in Kendra/Trikona in own/exaltation) and Jataka Parijata (benefics in Kendras, 8th/12th free). Horasara's definition is structurally different.

### Dhwaja Yoga
- **Formation:** Strong planets placed severally, one in another's sign, at least one in Lagna
- **Effects:** Fame, courage, wisdom, liked in royal circles, rich, fickle-minded
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 21–22
- **Cross-text notes:** Yavanas add: all planets in 10th and Lagna. Soma Jataka: all in Aries/Taurus/Pisces.

### Kundala Yoga
- **Formation:** Two strong planets in two different houses with 1–2 houses between them, others elsewhere
- **Effects:** Lives on others' money, irritable, satisfied with little, versed in arts, short-lived, few children
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 23–24

### Nabhasa Yogas (7 by sign-count)
- **Gola** (all 7 planets in 1 sign): short life, poor, defective limb, kills own children, no relations, always moving, banished — NOT a Raja Yoga
- **Yuga** (2 signs): disabled, harsh, shameless, indebted, short-lived, limited relatives — NOT auspicious
- **Shula** (3 signs): intelligent, many livelihoods, very sick, trader, two wives, childless — NOT auspicious
- **Kedara** (4 signs): helps relatives, earns via agriculture, dutiful, sluggish, honored — auspicious
- **Pasa** (5 signs): many expenses, expert, dear to all, intellectual, enthusiastic, royal favor — auspicious
- **Dama** (6 signs): happy, kind, devoted to welfare, noted scholar, wise, rich, warlike — auspicious
- **Veena** (7 signs): very learned, various wealth, chief of men, wise, expert, loves right things, enthusiastic — auspicious
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 25–33

### Kendra Yogas (Yavana)
- **Mangala Yoga:** All planets in Kendras from Lagna — auspicious acts, eloquent, issues, intelligent, long-lived
- **Madhya Yoga:** All planets specifically in 10th — lives abroad, troubled by relatives, timid, unstable wealth, few issues, dies badly
- **Kleeba Yoga:** All planets in 3rd/6th/12th — grieved, abroad, mean women, disputes, short-lived, dies abroad
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 34–38

### Rajju / Musala / Nala Yogas
- **Rajju:** All planets in movable signs — not industrious, poor, bad company, medium life
- **Musala:** All planets in fixed signs — energetic, wife/sons/wealth, wise, dear to own men
- **Nala:** All planets in dual signs — intelligent, disabled, few issues, helps others, rich, medium life
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 39–42

### Mala / Sarpa Yogas
- **Mala:** All benefics in Kendras — learned, wealthy
- **Sarpa:** All malefics in Kendras — penniless, talkative, leaves paternal place, troubles others, short-lived
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 43–45

### Shatpada / Varaha / Kantaka / Hala / Sringataka / Sakata / Vihaga Yogas
- **Shatpada:** All planets in 1st–6th signs — commands money, gathers essence of sastras
- **Varaha:** All planets in 1st, 5th, 4th — land, wealth, happiness, wisdom
- **Kantaka:** All planets in 2nd, 3rd, 4th — wealthy, learned, short-lived, issueless
- **Hala:** Lagna + 12th hold all planets — angry, poor, disabled, short-lived, farmer
- **Sringataka:** Lagna, 5th, 9th — windy, angry, great but poor
- **Sakata:** Lagna + 7th — moves like cart, bereaved of son/wife, wicked, idle, defective sight
- **Vihaga:** 4th + 10th — happy, amasses wealth, unsurpassed valor, many residences
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 46–52

### Sankha / Chakra / Samudra / Mahendra (Indra) Yogas
- **Sankha:** Planets in 9th, 11th, 1st, 4th — virtuous, brave, versed
- **Chakra:** All planets in 6 odd signs — repository of good qualities, king, large kingdom
- **Samudra:** Planets in 2nd, 4th, 6th, 8th, 10th, 12th (alternate signs) — blind from birth, dear to all, miser, wealthy, has issues
- **Mahendra (Indra):** Same but with two blank signs — famous, many titles, wealthy, superior
- **Source:** Ch 19, sloka 53–54

### Pushkala Yoga (Raja Yoga)
- **Formation:** Moon-sign lord in friendly Kendra along with Lagna lord, aspecting Lagna; Moon's dispositor strong
- **Effects:** Lords over earth; royal scion wears crown and umbrella
- **Source:** Ch 20, sloka 12–13
- **Cross-text notes:** Similar to Phaladeepika's Pushkala Yoga (Ch 6) but Phaladeepika requires a strong benefic aspecting Lagna rather than Moon's dispositor.

### Bhagya Yoga
- **Formation:** Day-born male with Lagna and all planets in odd signs and waxing Moon; OR night-born female with Lagna and all planets in even signs and waning Moon
- **Effects:** Scholar, auspicious deeds, knows celestial music, elephants, wealth; amplified by benefic in Lagna
- **Source:** Ch 20, sloka 26–27
- **Cross-text notes:** SEMANTICALLY SIMILAR TO Phaladeepika's Maha Bhagya Yoga (phaladeepika_yoga_mahabhagya). Difference: Phaladeepika requires Lagna/Sun/Moon all in odd (day-male) or even (night-female); Horasara requires all planets in odd/even + Moon waxing/waning. Same intent, different formulation.

### Other Notable Raja Yogas (Ch 20)
- Moon or Lagna in Vargothama aspected by 4+ planets → king (Ch 20:1)
- Venus in Ashwini Lagna aspected by all planets → king (Ch 20:2)
- 3+ planets in own/exaltation (avoiding Neecha Navamsa), one in Lagna → king (Ch 20:4–5)
- Moon + Mars in Lagna/9th/10th with strong Sun + Jupiter → king (Ch 20:6)
- Jupiter aspecting Mercury → king whose orders obeyed by other kings (Ch 20:7)
- Moon in friendly Navamsa aspected by Venus (night birth) → king (Ch 20:8)
- Moon in own/exaltation Navamsa aspected by Jupiter (day birth) → king (Ch 20:9)
- Leo Lagna at sunrise, Mercury in Virgo (avoiding Venus Navamsa) → even mean person becomes king (Ch 20:10)
- Lagna lord exalted in Kendra, not in Neecha/Shatru Navamsa, alone → Sarvabhauma (king of kings) (Ch 20:17)
- Mars in Aries/Leo/Sagittarius Lagna aspected by friend → king who destroys enemies with own hands (Ch 20:18)
- Mars in 10th in Ashwini/Anuradha/Dhanishta, OR Sun in Lagna (not Libra) → king (Ch 20:19)

### Jataka Bhanga (Avayoga) Yogas (Ch 24)
- **Jala Yoga:** Sun, Moon, Mars weak in Kendras/9th/12th → dunce, no wisdom/wealth, dependent, miserable
- **Kulapamsana Yoga:** Kendras with mixed planets, Moon aspected by Lagna lord or in Saturn's Navamsa → family desertion, penury, foreign
- **Bhikshashana Yoga:** All planets in Neecha Rasi/Navamsa, or exalted but not in 9th/10th → beggar
- **Chandala Yoga:** Mars + Saturn in Kendra, Rahu in Lagna, benefics in 12th/6th → abandons family duty
- **Unmada/Paisacha/Mahagada Yogas:** Various combinations producing insanity
- **Sanyasa Yogas:** Multiple combinations (Saturn aspecting Moon in benefic sign toward meridian; Jupiter/Sun/Moon in Lagna/10th with malefic in 12th aspected by strong Saturn; etc.) → ascetic orders
- **Critical nullifier:** Sun in Libra 10° (deep fall) cancels 1000 Raja Yogas (Ch 24:28); Venus in Virgo Navamsa destroys empire (Ch 24:29)

## Dasha Systems

- **Pindayurdaya:** Planets at deep exaltation contribute 19 (Sun), 25 (Moon), 15 (Mars), 12 (Mercury), 15 (Jupiter), 21 (Venus), 20 (Saturn) years; half at deep debilitation; proportionate between. Deductions for combustion, enemy signs, planetary war. Lagna contributes based on Navamsa attained. Used when Sun is strongest.

- **Amsayurdaya:** Navamsa-based; multiply planet longitude by 108, divide by 12 for year contribution. Multiplier 2× for own Navamsa/Drekkana/Rasi/Vargothama; 3× for retrograde/exalted; 1/2 for debilitated. Used when Lagna is strongest or all planets in own/exaltation Vargas.

- **Naisargikayu (Natural):** Used when Moon is strongest. Order: Moon→Mars→Mercury→Venus→Jupiter→Sun→Saturn (these prove auspicious in turn). Planets in evil Rasi/Amsa/eclipsed still yield good if they have potence; in enemy house/Amsa → kingdom.

- **Ashtakavarga Dasa:** Declared "best of all" (Ch 7 sloka 50). Uses Bhinnashtakavarga, Trikona Sodhana, Ekadhipatya Sodhana, Mandala Sodhana (cap 12 per sign). Multiply by Rasi multipliers (7,10,8,4,10,5,7,8,9,5,11,12 from Aries) and Graha multipliers (Jupiter=10, Mars=8, Venus=7, Mercury=5, others=5). Total × 7 ÷ 27 = years. Cap 100, multiply by 324 ÷ 365.

- **Jeevasarma's Method:** Longitudinal distance from deep debilitation × Pindayurdaya years ÷ 21600 = years; remainder × 12 ÷ 21600 = months; etc. Deductions: 1/7 if Lagna lord in 2nd/12th, planets near debilitation, weak, in Lagna/7th.

- **Vimshottari (Udu/Nakshatra) Dasa:** Total 120 years. Years: Sun 6, Moon 10, Mars 7, Rahu 18, Jupiter 16, Saturn 19, Mercury 17, Ketu 7, Venus 20. Counted from Krittika. Order from birth star (or Lagna star if Lagna stronger). End-periods of Sun/Mars/Saturn/Rahu bring disease, wealth-loss, disputes. Ketu dasa ~ Mars effects; Rahu ~ Saturn. Horasara declares it "acceptable to all" (Ch 31:11) but treats it briefly compared to other dashas.

- **Moola Dasha (for Antardasas):** Planet conjunct dasha lord gets 1/2; 5th/9th = 1/3; 7th = 1/7; 4th/8th = 1/4. Planets in 2/3/6/10/11/12 get no antardasha. Used for all sub-period predictions in Ch 18.

- **Naisargika Dasa (distinct from Naisargikayu):** Order Moon→Mars→Mercury→Venus→Jupiter→Sun→Saturn, each proving auspicious in their turn (Ch 8 sloka 9).

## Transit (Gochar) Rules

- **Saturn in 9th from Moon (with Sun or Moon dasa):** Father's death (Ch 17:27–28)
- **Saturn in 4th from Moon (or aspecting it from 7th/10th), before noon:** Native's death or distant travel (Ch 17:41)
- **Saturn transit to sign computed from 6th+8th+12th lords' longitude sum:** Brother's death (Ch 18:63)
- **Jupiter transit to 22nd decanate from Lagna or Moon (or trinal):** Native's death (Ch 18:64)
- **Jupiter transit to sign = (Jupiter + Rahu longitudes) sum, or trinal:** Native's death (Ch 18:65)
- **Sun transit to Dwadasamsa sign of 8th lord (or trinal), or to 8th lord's sign, or to 12th house:** Native's death (Ch 18:66)
- **Sun transit to sign = Sun_long × Rahu_long ÷ 21600 + natal Sun (Chanakya's method), or trinal:** Death (Ch 18:67–68)
- **Moon transit to 8th lord's sign from Moon (or trinal), or to sign = (Moon + 8th lord longitudes), or to that Navamsa:** Death (Ch 18:69)
- **Moon transit to natal Lagna / Moon / their Navamsas / 6th / 8th house:** Death (Ch 18:70)
- **Saturn + Mars in 6th/8th with Jupiter not in 1st/5th/9th, born in Parivesha kala:** Epilepsy (Ch 24:15)
- **Ashtakavarga-based:** Sign with 0 bindus in Sun's AV → avoid for marriage month; Moon transiting sign with 0 bindus in Moon's AV → avoid for auspicious events

## Ashtakavarga

- **Total bindus:** Sun=48, Moon=49, Mars=39, Mercury=54, Jupiter=56, Venus=52, Saturn=39; grand total = 337 across Sarvashtakavarga (Ch 17)
- **Reductions:** (1) Trikona Sodhana — within each trine set (Aries/Leo/Sagittarius, etc.), equalize to minimum; if one vacant, no change; if two vacant, third cleared; if all equal, all cleared. (2) Ekadhipatya Sodhana — for two signs sharing a lord (Mars: Aries/Scorpio; Mercury: Gemini/Virgo; Jupiter: Sagittarius/Pisces; Saturn: Capricorn/Aquarius; Venus: Taurus/Libra), apply second-stage reduction. Does NOT apply to Cancer and Leo (single-lord signs). (3) Mandala Sodhana — cap at 12 bindus per sign.
- **Multipliers:** Rasi (Aries→Pisces): 7, 10, 8, 4, 10, 5, 7, 8, 9, 5, 11, 12. Graha: Jupiter=10, Mars=8, Venus=7, Mercury=5, Sun/Moon/Saturn=5.
- **Applications:** Dasa effects (0 bindus = disease/loss; 4 = medium; 5+ = fulfillment); father's death timing; mother's death timing; profession direction; marriage month avoidance; progeny count (Jupiter's AV in 5th, minus dots from Neecha/Shatru planets); death month (multiply Sodhya Pinda by 8th-house bindus, divide by 12).

## Muhurta / Electional

Not directly addressed as a standalone chapter. However:
- **Avoid marriage month** corresponding to 0-bindu sign in Sun's Ashtakavarga (Ch 17:34)
- **Avoid auspicious events** when Moon transits 0-bindu sign in Moon's AV, or 8th-from-Moon nakshatras (Ch 17:37–38)
- **Gulika Kala** calculations given (Ch 24:5 notes): Day birth — Sunday 26 ghatis, Monday 22, Tuesday 18, Wednesday 14, Thursday 10, Friday 6, Saturday 2 (from sunrise); Night birth — Sunday 10, Monday 6, Tuesday 2, Wednesday 26, Thursday 22, Friday 18, Saturday 14 (from sunset)
- **Astrologer's muhurta for reading:** Begin when Moon and her nakshatra are beneficially disposed; 2nd house well-placed; Jupiter unafflicted; no rains/eclipse/thunder (Ch 32:18)

## Cross-Text Relations

- **Agrees with BPHS on:**
  - Sunapha/Anapha/Durudhura lunar yogas (formation + effects)
  - Adhi Yoga
  - Kemadruma Yoga (core definition)
  - Vesi/Vosi/Ubhayachari solar yogas (though Horasara spells "Vasi" where BPHS uses "Vosi")
  - Raja Yoga principles (exalted planets, Kendra/Trikona lords)
  - Pancha Mahapurusha concept (implicit in planetary dignity treatment)
  - 9 grahas with their natural significations
  - Exaltation/debilitation degrees (identical to BPHS)

- **Differs from BPHS on:**
  - **Vimshottari primacy:** BPHS makes Vimshottari the primary dasha; Horasara devotes most chapters to Pindayurdaya/Amsayurdaya/Ashtakavarga/Moola dashas and treats Vimshottari only in the brief Ch 31 (translator's note to Ch 8 explicitly remarks on this)
  - **Ashtakavarga supremacy:** Horasara declares Ashtakavarga dasa "best of all" (Ch 7:50); BPHS does not elevate AV to this extent
  - **Spelling/terminology:** "Durudhura" (Horasara) vs "Duradhura" (BPHS); "Vasi/Vesi" (Horasara) vs "Vosi/Vesi" (BPHS)
  - **Kemadruma cancellation:** Horasara adds a variant Kemadruma (9th lord in 12th, 12th lord in 2nd weak, malefics in 3rd) not in BPHS

- **Agrees with Phaladeepika on:**
  - Kemadruma Yoga definition (Moon without flanking planets)
  - Adhi Yoga
  - Pushkala Yoga (similar but variant conditions)
  - Sakata Yoga (Ch 24 implicit)
  - Pancha Mahapurusha yogas (Ruchaka, Bhadra, Hamsa, Malavya, Shasha — implicit in planetary dignity treatment, though not named as such)
  - Death cause from 8th house occupant
  - Vimshottari primacy for predictive dasha (Phaladeepika Ch 19–21)

- **Contradicts Phaladeepika on:**
  - **Parvatha Yoga:** Horasara (planets in 1/7/10/4, none in 12/8, 9th occupied) CONTRADICTS Phaladeepika Ch 6:35 (Lagna lord in Kendra/Trikona in own/exaltation) — completely different definition
  - **Maha Bhagya / Bhagya Yoga:** Phaladeepika requires Lagna/Sun/Moon all in odd (day-male) or even (night-female) signs; Horasara's Bhagya Yoga requires all planets in odd/even + waxing/waning Moon — different formulation, same intent
  - **Vimshottari emphasis:** Phaladeepika places Vimshottari FIRST among dashas (Ch 19, 21); Horasara defers it to Ch 31 (translator explicitly notes this contrast)

- **Supplements GPD (Gochar Phaladeepika) on:**
  - Death-timing transit rules: Saturn in 9th/4th from Moon, Jupiter in 22nd decanate, Sun in 8th lord's Dwadasamsa, Chanakya's Sun×Rahu method — Horasara provides computational methods that complement GPD's transit rules
  - Ashtakavarga-based transit timing (multiply Sodhya Pinda by house bindus, divide by 27 for nakshatra or 12 for sign) — unique computational framework GPD does not provide
  - Father/mother death timing via AV + Saturn/Sun transits

- **Unique to this text:**
  - Six-fold Arohini/Avarohini classification of Moon dasa (ocean-wave criterion unique)
  - Marana Pada (Arudha of 8th) for death-timing
  - Moola dasha antardasa allocation scheme (1/2, 1/3, 1/7, 1/4 by position)
  - Sauramana conversion tables for Vimshottari (Manitha/Kesava method)
  - Chanakya's Sun×Rahu death-timing formula
  - 12-sign × 5-Trimsamsa-lord matrix for female horoscopy (most elaborate in classical literature)
  - Critical years per Navamsa (e.g., Aries Navamsa: 12/25/50/65; Taurus: 22/10/23/72; etc.)
  - Gandanta nakshatra survival = kingship (with historical examples: Dalmia, Gandhi)
  - Kirana (ray) values for 12 signs (9,8,14,8,11,9,6,7,5,10,8,14) and 7 planets (20,18,12,14,8,18,10) used in Nashta Jataka

## Conflicts & Ambiguities

- **Parvatha Yoga definition:** Horasara (Ch 19:19 — planets in 1/7/10/4 + 9th occupied, 8th/12th vacant) CONTRADICTS Phaladeepika (Ch 6:35 — Lagna lord in Kendra/Trikona in own/exaltation) and Jataka Parijata (benefics in Kendras, 8th/12th free OR Lagna/12th lords in mutual angles aspected by friend). Three texts, three different definitions of the same-named yoga.

- **Debilitated retrograde planet status:** Horasara Ch 3 sloka 13 note explicitly states the author does NOT subscribe to the view that a debilitated retrograde planet equals exaltation (some texts hold this view). The author holds that a debilitated planet in retrograde is NOT strong, except in Capricorn where Jupiter is in fall.

- **Vimshottari primacy:** Horasara's structure (defers Vimshottari to Ch 31; translator's note to Ch 8: "Unfortunately, direct and sufficient stress is not made in this work on Udu dasa or Vimshottari Dasa, as the chapter concerned has only a couple of slokas, and is placed at the end of the work") CONTRADICTS the prevailing classical consensus (Phaladeepika, later texts) that Vimshottari is primary. Yet Ch 31:11 declares Vimshottari "acceptable to all" — internal tension.

- **Rahu/Ketu dignities:** Horasara Ch 2 sloka 8 note lists 6+ different classical opinions without fixing one: Vriddha Samhita (Aquarius/Scorpio own), Jatakalankaram (Taurus/Scorpio exaltation for both), Jatakachintamani (Rahu Taurus exaltation, Ketu Gemini), Syama Sangraham (Rahu Gemini/Sagittarius), Sarvartha Chintamani (Rahu Taurus/Scorpio, Ketu Scorpio/Taurus), Bhavartha Ratnakara (Rahu Taurus, Ketu Scorpio, with Moolatrikona Gemini/Cancer for Rahu and Sagittarius/Capricorn for Ketu). Text acknowledges unresolvable variance.

- **Kemadruma cancellation by Kendra occupation:** Varaha Mihira (Brihat Jataka) quotes Srutakirti and Jeevasarma that only 4th/10th from Moon (not 2nd/12th) should be considered, and that Moon's Navamsa 2nd/12th should also be considered — but Varaha Mihira and Prithuyasas reject both views. Disagreement among authorities.

- **Sun in deep fall nullification:** Horasara Ch 24:28 states Sun in Libra 10° cancels 1000 Raja Yogas — Jataka Parijata adds the condition "aspected by malefics" which Horasara does NOT require. Stricter view in Horasara.

- **Trimsamsa division values:** Horasara Ch 1:16 gives Trimsamsa as Mars 5, Saturn 5, Jupiter 8, Mercury 7, Venus 5 (odd signs); reverse for even. Matches BPHS but differs from some later texts in application specifics.
